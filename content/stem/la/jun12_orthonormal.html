Orthonormal Basis
Lin Alg Final25 June 2026
Quick reference. An orthonormal basis \(\{q_i\}\) satisfies \(\langle q_i,q_j\rangle=\delta_{ij}\). Coordinates become trivial: \(v=\sum\langle v,q_i\rangle q_i\). Orthonormal \(Q\) has \(Q^TQ=I\).
★ Connected ProblemFrom Gram–Schmidt to least squaresexam-style
In \(\mathbb R^3\), let \(a_1=(1,1,0),\ a_2=(1,0,1)\) and target \(b=(2,1,1)\).
chains: inner product · Gram–Schmidt · projection · least squares
  1. (a)
    Orthogonalise \(\{a_1,a_2\}\) by Gram–Schmidt.
    Solution\(u_1=(1,1,0)\); \(u_2=a_2-\tfrac{\langle a_2,u_1\rangle}{\langle u_1,u_1\rangle}u_1=(1,0,1)-\tfrac12(1,1,0)=(\tfrac12,-\tfrac12,1)\).
    orthogonal \(\{u_1,u_2\}\).
  2. (b)
    Project \(b\) onto \(u_1\).
    Solution\(\tfrac{\langle b,u_1\rangle}{\langle u_1,u_1\rangle}u_1=\tfrac{3}{2}(1,1,0)=(\tfrac32,\tfrac32,0)\).
    \((\tfrac32,\tfrac32,0)\).
  3. (c)
    Set up the least-squares normal equations for \(A\mathbf x=b\) with \(A=[a_1\ a_2]\).
    Solution\(A^TA=\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\1&2\end{pmatrix},\ A^Tb=\begin{pmatrix}3\\3\end{pmatrix}\); solve \(A^TA\hat{\mathbf x}=A^Tb\).
    \(\hat{\mathbf x}=(1,1)\).
  4. (d)
    What does \(A\hat{\mathbf x}\) represent?
    SolutionThe orthogonal projection of \(b\) onto \(\operatorname{Col}(A)\); the residual \(b-A\hat{\mathbf x}\perp\operatorname{Col}(A)\).
    projection of \(b\).
Problem 1Build orthonormal basis12 pts
Orthonormalise \(\{(1,1,0),(1,0,1)\}\) (Gram–Schmidt + normalise).
Solution\(u_1=(1,1,0)\to\hat q_1=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt2}(1,1,0)\). \(u_2=(\tfrac12,-\tfrac12,1)\), \(\lVert u_2\rVert=\sqrt{3/2}\Rightarrow\hat q_2=\sqrt{\tfrac23}(\tfrac12,-\tfrac12,1)\).
\(\{\hat q_1,\hat q_2\}\) orthonormal.
Problem 2Coordinates via dot products8 pts
With an orthonormal basis \(\{q_1,q_2\}\), how do you get the coordinates of \(v\)?
SolutionJust dot: the \(i\)-th coordinate is \(\langle v,q_i\rangle\). No system to solve — that's the power of orthonormality.
coord\(_i=\langle v,q_i\rangle\).
