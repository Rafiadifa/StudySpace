Powers, Markov & Linear ODEs
Lin Alg Final25 June 2026
Quick reference. Diagonalisation powers everything: \(A^k=PD^kP^{-1}\). Markov: a stochastic matrix has \(\lambda=1\); the steady state is its eigenvector (normalised to sum 1). Linear ODE \(\mathbf y'=A\mathbf y\) has solution \(\mathbf y=\sum c_i e^{\lambda_i t}\mathbf v_i\).
★ Connected ProblemMarkov chain: steady state & convergenceexam-style
A 2-state chain has column-stochastic transition matrix \(P=\begin{pmatrix}3/4&1/2\\1/4&1/2\end{pmatrix}\).
chains: stochastic ⇒ \(\lambda=1\) · steady-state eigenvector · second eigenvalue · convergence rate
  1. (a)
    Confirm the columns sum to 1, and explain why \(\lambda=1\) must be an eigenvalue.
    SolutionBoth columns sum to \(1\). Then the all-ones row vector satisfies \(\mathbf 1^{\mathsf T}P=\mathbf 1^{\mathsf T}\), so \(1\) is an eigenvalue of \(P^{\mathsf T}\) — hence of \(P\).
    \(\lambda=1\) guaranteed.
  2. (b)
    Find the steady-state distribution \(\boldsymbol\pi\) (solve \(P\boldsymbol\pi=\boldsymbol\pi\), normalise).
    Solution\((P-I)\boldsymbol\pi=0\): \(-\tfrac14\pi_1+\tfrac12\pi_2=0\Rightarrow\pi_1=2\pi_2\). Normalise \(\pi_1+\pi_2=1\Rightarrow\boldsymbol\pi=(\tfrac23,\tfrac13)\).
    \(\boldsymbol\pi=(\tfrac23,\tfrac13)\).
  3. (c)
    Find the other eigenvalue.
    Solution\(\operatorname{tr}P=\tfrac54\) and one eigenvalue is \(1\), so the other is \(\tfrac54-1=\tfrac14\) (check \(\det P=\tfrac38-\tfrac18=\tfrac14=1\cdot\tfrac14\) ✓).
    \(\lambda_2=\tfrac14\).
  4. (d)
    Starting from any distribution \(\mathbf x_0\), what happens to \(P^n\mathbf x_0\), and how fast?
    SolutionWrite \(\mathbf x_0=\boldsymbol\pi+c\,\mathbf v_2\); then \(P^n\mathbf x_0=\boldsymbol\pi+c(\tfrac14)^n\mathbf v_2\to\boldsymbol\pi\). The transient decays like \((\tfrac14)^n\) — geometric, set by the second eigenvalue.
    \(\to\boldsymbol\pi=(\tfrac23,\tfrac13)\) at rate \((1/4)^n\).
Problem 1Steady state12 pts
Markov matrix \(P=\begin{pmatrix}0.8&0.3\\0.2&0.7\end{pmatrix}\). Find the steady-state distribution.
SolutionSolve \(P\mathbf s=\mathbf s\): \((P-I)=\begin{pmatrix}-0.2&0.3\\0.2&-0.3\end{pmatrix}\Rightarrow 0.2s_1=0.3s_2\Rightarrow s=(3,2)\) up to scale; normalise: \((0.6,0.4)\).
Steady state \((0.6,0.4)\).
Problem 2Linear ODE10 pts
Solve \(\mathbf y'=A\mathbf y\) with \(A=\begin{pmatrix}1&2\\2&1\end{pmatrix}\).
SolutionEigen: \(\lambda=3,(1,1);\ \lambda=-1,(1,-1)\). \(\mathbf y=c_1 e^{3t}(1,1)+c_2 e^{-t}(1,-1)\).
\(\mathbf y=c_1e^{3t}(1,1)+c_2e^{-t}(1,-1)\).
Each eigenpair contributes a mode \(e^{\lambda t}\mathbf v\); sign of \(\lambda\) sets growth vs decay.
