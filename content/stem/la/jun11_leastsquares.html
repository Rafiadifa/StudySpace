Projections & Least Squares
Lin Alg Final25 June 2026
Quick reference. Least-squares solution of \(Ax=b\) (inconsistent) solves the normal equations \(A^TA\hat x=A^Tb\). The projection of \(b\) onto \(\mathrm{Col}(A)\) is \(A\hat x\). Residual \(b-A\hat x\perp\mathrm{Col}(A)\).
★ Connected ProblemFrom Gram–Schmidt to least squaresexam-style
In \(\mathbb R^3\), let \(a_1=(1,1,0),\ a_2=(1,0,1)\) and target \(b=(2,1,1)\).
chains: inner product · Gram–Schmidt · projection · least squares
  1. (a)
    Orthogonalise \(\{a_1,a_2\}\) by Gram–Schmidt.
    Solution\(u_1=(1,1,0)\); \(u_2=a_2-\tfrac{\langle a_2,u_1\rangle}{\langle u_1,u_1\rangle}u_1=(1,0,1)-\tfrac12(1,1,0)=(\tfrac12,-\tfrac12,1)\).
    orthogonal \(\{u_1,u_2\}\).
  2. (b)
    Project \(b\) onto \(u_1\).
    Solution\(\tfrac{\langle b,u_1\rangle}{\langle u_1,u_1\rangle}u_1=\tfrac{3}{2}(1,1,0)=(\tfrac32,\tfrac32,0)\).
    \((\tfrac32,\tfrac32,0)\).
  3. (c)
    Set up the least-squares normal equations for \(A\mathbf x=b\) with \(A=[a_1\ a_2]\).
    Solution\(A^TA=\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\1&2\end{pmatrix},\ A^Tb=\begin{pmatrix}3\\3\end{pmatrix}\); solve \(A^TA\hat{\mathbf x}=A^Tb\).
    \(\hat{\mathbf x}=(1,1)\).
  4. (d)
    What does \(A\hat{\mathbf x}\) represent?
    SolutionThe orthogonal projection of \(b\) onto \(\operatorname{Col}(A)\); the residual \(b-A\hat{\mathbf x}\perp\operatorname{Col}(A)\).
    projection of \(b\).
Problem 1Fit a line15 pts
Least-squares line through \((0,1),(1,1),(2,3)\). Set up and solve the normal equations.
Solution\(A=\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\1&1\\1&2\end{pmatrix},b=\begin{pmatrix}1\\1\\3\end{pmatrix}\). \(A^TA=\begin{pmatrix}3&3\\3&5\end{pmatrix}\), \(A^Tb=\begin{pmatrix}5\\7\end{pmatrix}\). Solve: \(\hat x=(\tfrac23,1)\).
Line \(y=\tfrac23+x\).
Check: \(A^TA\) is \(2\times2\), invertible since columns of \(A\) are independent.
Problem 2Why normal equations?8 pts
Why does \(A^TA\hat x=A^Tb\) give the best fit?
SolutionThe residual \(b-A\hat x\) must be orthogonal to every column of \(A\), i.e. \(A^T(b-A\hat x)=0\), which rearranges to the normal equations. Orthogonality = minimal distance.
Residual ⟂ Col(A) ⇔ normal equations.
