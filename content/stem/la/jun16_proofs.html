Proof 1Eigenvectors for distinct eigenvalues are independent
SolutionSuppose \(\sum c_i v_i=0\) with distinct \(\lambda_i\). Apply \((A-\lambda_2 I)\cdots\) to annihilate all but one term, forcing that \(c_i=0\); induct. Hence independent.
Proof 2Orthogonal vectors are independent
SolutionIf \(\sum c_i v_i=0\) with \(v_i\) mutually orthogonal, dot with \(v_j\): \(c_j\lVert v_j\rVert^2=0\Rightarrow c_j=0\). Independent.
Proof 3\(\det(AB)=\det A\det B\) (idea)
SolutionDeterminant is the unique alternating multilinear function normalised to \(\det I=1\); both sides agree as such functions of the columns of \(B\). (Or: reduce via elementary matrices.)
