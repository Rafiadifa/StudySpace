Prove It Cold

Final · 25 Jun
Recall 1State the theorem
⌁ Write it from memory
State the Rank–Nullity Theorem precisely. Define every symbol: what are the rank, the nullity, and what does \(n\) refer to?
Model For a linear map \(T:V\to W\) with \(\dim V=n\): \(\operatorname{rank}(T)+\operatorname{nullity}(T)=n\), where \(\operatorname{rank}(T)=\dim(\operatorname{im}T)\) and \(\operatorname{nullity}(T)=\dim(\ker T)\). For a matrix \(A\in\mathbb R^{m\times n}\): \(\dim(\operatorname{Col}A)+\dim(\operatorname{Nul}A)=n\) — \(n\) is the number of columns.
The most-missed detail: \(n\) is the dimension of the domain (columns), not the codomain.
Recall 2Build the proof
⌁ Reconstruct the argument
Prove it. Start from a basis of the kernel and extend. What do you need to show about the images of the extension vectors?
Model proof Let \(\{u_1,\dots,u_k\}\) be a basis of \(\ker T\) (so \(\operatorname{nullity}=k\)). Extend to a basis \(\{u_1,\dots,u_k,w_1,\dots,w_{n-k}\}\) of \(V\).
Claim: \(\{T(w_1),\dots,T(w_{n-k})\}\) is a basis of \(\operatorname{im}T\).
Spanning: any \(T(v)\) expands via the basis; the \(u_i\) terms vanish under \(T\), leaving a combination of the \(T(w_j)\).
Independence: if \(\sum c_j T(w_j)=0\), then \(T(\sum c_j w_j)=0\), so \(\sum c_j w_j\in\ker T\) is a combination of the \(u_i\); independence of the full basis forces all \(c_j=0\).
Hence \(\operatorname{rank}=n-k\), so \(\operatorname{rank}+\operatorname{nullity}=(n-k)+k=n\). \(\blacksquare\)
The engine of the proof: extend a kernel basis, then show the images of the extension vectors form a basis of the image.
Recall 3Apply it fast
⌁ One-line answers
A matrix \(A\) is \(3\times5\) with rank 2. (a) nullity? (b) Is \(Ax=b\) ever unique? (c) Can the columns span \(\mathbb R^3\)?
Model (a) nullity \(=5-2=3\). (b) No — nullity \(>0\) means the homogeneous system has free variables, so solutions are never unique. (c) No — column space has dimension 2 \(<3\), so the columns cannot span \(\mathbb R^3\).
nullity 3; never unique; cannot span \(\mathbb R^3\).
