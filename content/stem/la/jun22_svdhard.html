Lin Alg Final25 June 2026
SVD & least squares. Singular values \(\sigma_i=\sqrt{\text{eigenvalues of }A^{\mathsf T}A}\); right singular vectors \(=\) eigenvectors of \(A^{\mathsf T}A\), left \(u_i=Av_i/\sigma_i\). Normal equations \(A^{\mathsf T}A\,\hat{\mathbf x}=A^{\mathsf T}\mathbf b\). Projection onto \(\operatorname{Col}(A)\): \(P=A(A^{\mathsf T}A)^{-1}A^{\mathsf T}\). \(\mathbf b\in\operatorname{Ran}(A)\iff A\mathbf x=\mathbf b\) is consistent. For the unit circle \(S\), the image \(B(S)\) is an ellipse with semi-axes \(=\sigma_i(B)\) along the left singular vectors \(u_i\).
★ Connected ProblemSignal recovery: SVD, projection, least squarespast-final · hard
Recover \(\mathbf x\in\mathbb R^2\) from \(\mathbf b\in\mathbb R^3\) via \(A\mathbf x=\mathbf b\): \(A=\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\1&0\\1&1\end{pmatrix},\ \mathbf b=\begin{pmatrix}3\\2\\t\end{pmatrix}\) (noisy).
  1. (a)
    Find the singular values of \(A\).
    Solution\(A^{\mathsf T}A=\begin{pmatrix}3&2\\2&2\end{pmatrix}\), \(\operatorname{tr}=5,\det=2\Rightarrow\lambda=\tfrac{5\pm\sqrt{17}}{2}\). \(\sigma_{1,2}=\sqrt{\tfrac{5\pm\sqrt{17}}{2}}\approx 2.14,\ 0.66\).
    \(\sigma=\sqrt{\tfrac{5\pm\sqrt{17}}{2}}\).
  2. (b)
    For which \(t\) is \(\mathbf b\notin\operatorname{Ran}(A)\)?
    Solution\(\operatorname{Ran}(A)=\operatorname{span}\{(1,1,1),(1,0,1)\}\); a combo \(a(1,1,1)+c(1,0,1)=(a+c,\,a,\,a+c)\). Matching \(\mathbf b\): \(a=2,\ a+c=3\Rightarrow c=1\), forcing the 3rd entry \(=3\). So \(\mathbf b\in\operatorname{Ran}(A)\iff t=3\); hence \(\mathbf b\notin\operatorname{Ran}(A)\iff t\neq3\).
    \(t\neq3\).
  3. (c)
    Find the projection matrix \(P\) onto \(\operatorname{Ran}(A)\).
    Solution\((A^{\mathsf T}A)^{-1}=\tfrac12\begin{pmatrix}2&-2\\-2&3\end{pmatrix}\); \(P=A(A^{\mathsf T}A)^{-1}A^{\mathsf T}=\begin{pmatrix}\tfrac12&0&\tfrac12\\0&1&0\\\tfrac12&0&\tfrac12\end{pmatrix}\). (Symmetric, \(P^2=P\), \(\operatorname{tr}P=2=\dim\operatorname{Ran}(A)\).)
    as shown.
  4. (d)
    Find the least-squares solution \(\hat{\mathbf x}\).
    Solution\(A^{\mathsf T}\mathbf b=(5+t,\ 3+t)\); \(\hat{\mathbf x}=(A^{\mathsf T}A)^{-1}A^{\mathsf T}\mathbf b=\tfrac12\begin{pmatrix}2&-2\\-2&3\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}5+t\\3+t\end{pmatrix}=\big(2,\ \tfrac{t-1}{2}\big)\).
    \(\hat{\mathbf x}=(2,\tfrac{t-1}{2})\).
  5. (e)
    (Hard) Let \(B=\begin{pmatrix}2&2\\1&0\\0&1\end{pmatrix}\). \(B\) maps the unit circle to an ellipse in \(\mathbb R^3\): find the semi-axis lengths and directions.
    Solution\(B^{\mathsf T}B=\begin{pmatrix}5&4\\4&5\end{pmatrix}\), eigenvalues \(9,1\Rightarrow\sigma=3,1\). Right vecs \(v_1=\tfrac1{\sqrt2}(1,1),v_2=\tfrac1{\sqrt2}(1,-1)\); left \(u_i=Bv_i/\sigma_i\): \(u_1=\tfrac{1}{3\sqrt2}(4,1,1)\), \(u_2=\tfrac1{\sqrt2}(0,1,-1)\). Long axis: length \(3\) along \((4,1,1)\); short axis: length \(1\) along \((0,1,-1)\).
    axes \(3\) & \(1\) along \((4,1,1),(0,1,-1)\).
Problem 1Least-squares line fitwarm-up
Find the best-fit line \(y=mx+c\) through \((0,1),(1,3),(2,4)\).
Solution\(A=\begin{pmatrix}0&1\\1&1\\2&1\end{pmatrix},\mathbf b=(1,3,4)\). \(A^{\mathsf T}A=\begin{pmatrix}5&3\\3&3\end{pmatrix},A^{\mathsf T}\mathbf b=(11,8)\), \(\det=6\). \(\hat{\mathbf x}=\tfrac16\begin{pmatrix}3&-3\\-3&5\end{pmatrix}(11,8)=\tfrac16(9,7)=(\tfrac32,\tfrac76)\).
\(y=\tfrac32 x+\tfrac76\).
Problem 2Projection & residualmedium
Project \(\mathbf b=(1,1,1)\) onto the plane \(\operatorname{span}\{(1,0,1),(0,1,1)\}\); give the residual.
Solution\(A=\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\0&1\\1&1\end{pmatrix}\), \(A^{\mathsf T}A=\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\1&2\end{pmatrix}\), \(A^{\mathsf T}\mathbf b=(2,2)\), \(\hat{\mathbf x}=\tfrac13\begin{pmatrix}2&-1\\-1&2\end{pmatrix}(2,2)=(\tfrac23,\tfrac23)\). Projection \(=A\hat{\mathbf x}=(\tfrac23,\tfrac23,\tfrac43)\); residual \(\mathbf b-A\hat{\mathbf x}=(\tfrac13,\tfrac13,-\tfrac13)\perp\) plane.
proj \((\tfrac23,\tfrac23,\tfrac43)\), residual \((\tfrac13,\tfrac13,-\tfrac13)\).
