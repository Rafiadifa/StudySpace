State each theorem precisely AND give the one-line idea of its proof. No notes. Reveal to check.
Theorem 1Rank–Nullity
Solution\(\dim(\mathrm{im}T)+\dim(\ker T)=\dim V\). Idea: extend a kernel basis; images of the extension vectors form a basis of the image.
Theorem 2Spectral Theorem
SolutionReal symmetric \(A=Q\Lambda Q^T\), \(Q\) orthonormal eigenvectors, real \(\lambda\). Idea: distinct-eigenvalue eigenvectors are orthogonal; repeated ones can be orthonormalised.
Theorem 3Invertible Matrix Theorem
SolutionFor square \(A\): invertible ⇔ \(\det\neq0\) ⇔ rank \(n\) ⇔ \(\mathrm{Nul}A=\{0\}\) ⇔ columns independent ⇔ \(\lambda=0\) not an eigenvalue. Idea: all are equivalent restatements of "full rank".
Theorem 4Gram–Schmidt / QR
SolutionAny independent set has an orthonormal set with the same span; matrix form \(A=QR\), \(Q\) orthonormal, \(R\) upper-triangular. Idea: subtract projections onto previous vectors.
