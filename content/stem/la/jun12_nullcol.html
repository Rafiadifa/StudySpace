Quick reference. Row-reduce \(A\). Column space basis = original columns at pivot positions; \(\dim=\)rank. Null space = solutions of \(Ax=0\); basis from free variables; \(\dim=n-\)rank. (Rank–Nullity.)
★ Connected ProblemSubspace → basis → rank-nullity → coordinatesexam-style
Let \(A=\begin{pmatrix}1&2&1\\3&6&4\end{pmatrix}\).
chains: column/null space · basis · rank–nullity · coordinates
  1. (a)
    Row-reduce and give bases for \(\operatorname{Col}(A)\) and \(\operatorname{Nul}(A)\).
    SolutionRREF \(\to\begin{pmatrix}1&2&0\\0&0&1\end{pmatrix}\). Pivots in cols 1,3 \(\Rightarrow\) Col basis \(\{(1,3),(1,4)\}\); free \(x_2\): \(\operatorname{Nul}=\operatorname{span}\{(-2,1,0)\}\).
    rank 2, nullity 1.
  2. (b)
    Verify rank–nullity.
    Solution\(2+1=3=\) number of columns ✓.
    \(2+1=3\).
  3. (c)
    Is \(b=(0,1)\) in \(\operatorname{Col}(A)\)? If so, give one solution of \(A\mathbf x=b\).
    SolutionTwo independent columns \(\Rightarrow\operatorname{Col}(A)=\mathbb R^2\), so yes. \(b=(0,1)=\text{col}_3-\text{col}_1\Rightarrow\mathbf x=(-1,0,1)\).
    \(\mathbf x=(-1,0,1)\).

\(A=\begin{pmatrix}1&2&1\\2&4&3\end{pmatrix}\). Bases for Col and Nul.
SolutionRREF: \(R_2-2R_1=(0,0,1)\Rightarrow\begin{pmatrix}1&2&0\\0&0&1\end{pmatrix}\). Pivots in cols 1,3 ⇒ Col basis \(\{(1,2),(1,3)\}\), rank 2. Free var \(x_2\): \(x_1=-2x_2,x_3=0\) ⇒ Nul basis \(\{(-2,1,0)\}\).
rank 2; Col \(=\{(1,2),(1,3)\}\); Nul \(=\{(-2,1,0)\}\).
