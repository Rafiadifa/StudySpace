Basis & Dimension

Final · 25 Jun
Quick reference. A basis of \(V\) is a linearly independent spanning set. To test independence of vectors, form a matrix (as columns) and row-reduce — independent iff every column is a pivot column. \(\dim V\) = number of vectors in any basis = number of pivots. For a subspace defined by equations, the dimension drops by the number of independent constraints.

    rank 2, nullity 1.

    Verify rank–nullity.
    Solution\(2+1=3=\) number of columns ✓.
    \(2+1=3\).
  3. (c)

    Solution\(b=(1,2)\) is the first column, so yes; \(\mathbf x=(1,0,0)\) works.

Problem 1Independence test6 pts
Are \(\mathbf v_1=(1,2,3),\ \mathbf v_2=(2,5,7),\ \mathbf v_3=(1,3,5)\) linearly independent in \(\mathbb R^3\)?
Solution Row-reduce \(\begin{pmatrix}1&2&1\\2&5&3\\3&7&5\end{pmatrix}\). \(R_2-2R_1=(0,1,1)\), \(R_3-3R_1=(0,1,2)\), then \(R_3-R_2=(0,0,1)\). Three pivots.
Independent — they form a basis of \(\mathbb R^3\).
Problem 2Dependent set6 pts
Show \(\mathbf v_1=(1,1,0),\ \mathbf v_2=(0,1,1),\ \mathbf v_3=(1,2,1)\) are dependent and give the relation.
Solution Notice \(\mathbf v_1+\mathbf v_2=(1,2,1)=\mathbf v_3\). So \(\mathbf v_1+\mathbf v_2-\mathbf v_3=\mathbf 0\).
Dependent; \(\mathbf v_3=\mathbf v_1+\mathbf v_2\). A basis of their span is \(\{\mathbf v_1,\mathbf v_2\}\), dimension 2.
Problem 3Basis from a spanning set8 pts
From \(\{(1,0,1),(2,0,2),(0,1,1),(1,1,0)\}\) extract a basis for the span.
Solution Put as columns and row-reduce; the pivot columns pick the basis. Column 2 \(=2\times\)column 1 (dependent). Columns 1, 3, 4 are independent and span \(\mathbb R^3\).
Basis: \(\{(1,0,1),(0,1,1),(1,1,0)\}\), dimension 3.
Keep the original columns at pivot positions — not the reduced ones — when reporting a basis of the span.
Problem 4Subspace dimension8 pts
Find a basis and the dimension of \(W=\{(x,y,z,w)\in\mathbb R^4 : x+y+z+w=0,\ x-z=0\}\).
Solution From \(x=z\) and \(w=-x-y-z=-2x-y\). Free variables \(x,y\): \((x,y,z,w)=x(1,0,1,-2)+y(0,1,0,-1)\).
Basis \(\{(1,0,1,-2),(0,1,0,-1)\}\), \(\dim W=2\).
Two independent constraints on \(\mathbb R^4\) ⇒ dimension \(4-2=2\). Always re-check the constraints are independent.
Problem 5Polynomial space8 pts
In \(P_2\) (polynomials of degree \(\le2\)), are \(1+x,\ x+x^2,\ 1+x^2\) a basis?
Solution Use coordinates in \(\{1,x,x^2\}\): \((1,1,0),(0,1,1),(1,0,1)\). Determinant \(=1(1)-0+1(-1)=\dots\) compute: \(\det\begin{pmatrix}1&0&1\\1&1&0\\0&1&1\end{pmatrix}=1(1)-0+1(1)=2\neq0\).
Nonzero determinant ⇒ independent; 3 vectors in 3-dim \(P_2\) ⇒ a basis.
Abstract vector spaces reduce to \(\mathbb R^n\) once you fix a coordinate basis — then it's ordinary matrix work.
