Change of Basis

Final · 25 Jun
Quick reference. The coordinate vector \([\mathbf v]_B\) lists the coefficients of \(\mathbf v\) in basis \(B\). The change-of-basis matrix \(P_{B}\) (columns = basis vectors of \(B\) in standard coords) satisfies \(\mathbf v=P_B[\mathbf v]_B\), so \([\mathbf v]_B=P_B^{-1}\mathbf v\). Between two bases: \([\mathbf v]_C=P_C^{-1}P_B[\mathbf v]_B\); the matrix \(P_{C\leftarrow B}=P_C^{-1}P_B\).

Let \(A=\begin{pmatrix}1&2&3\\2&4&8\end{pmatrix}\).
chains: column/null space · basis · rank–nullity · coordinates

    Row-reduce and give bases for \(\operatorname{Col}(A)\) and \(\operatorname{Nul}(A)\).
    SolutionRREF \(\to\begin{pmatrix}1&2&0\\0&0&1\end{pmatrix}\). Pivots in cols 1,3 ⇒ Col basis \(\{(1,2),(3,8)\}\); free \(x_2\): \(\operatorname{Nul}=\operatorname{span}\{(-2,1,0)\}\).
    rank 2, nullity 1.
  2. (b)
    Verify rank–nullity.
    Solution\(2+1=3=\) number of columns ✓.
    \(2+1=3\).
  3. (c)
    Is \(b=(1,2)\) in \(\operatorname{Col}(A)\)? If so, give one solution of \(A\mathbf x=b\).
    Solution\(b=(1,2)\) is the first column, so yes; \(\mathbf x=(1,0,0)\) works.
    \(\mathbf x=(1,0,0)\).
Problem 1Coordinates in a basis8 pts
Basis \(B=\{(1,1),(1,-1)\}\) of \(\mathbb R^2\). Find \([\mathbf v]_B\) for \(\mathbf v=(4,2)\).
Solution Solve \(a(1,1)+b(1,-1)=(4,2)\): \(a+b=4,\ a-b=2\Rightarrow a=3,\ b=1\).
\([\mathbf v]_B=(3,1)\).
Problem 2Build P and invert10 pts
For the same \(B\), write \(P_B\) and \(P_B^{-1}\), and verify \([\mathbf v]_B=P_B^{-1}\mathbf v\) for \(\mathbf v=(4,2)\).
Solution \(P_B=\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\1&-1\end{pmatrix}\), \(\det=-2\), \(P_B^{-1}=\dfrac{1}{-2}\begin{pmatrix}-1&-1\\-1&1\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}1/2&1/2\\1/2&-1/2\end{pmatrix}\). \(P_B^{-1}(4,2)^T=(3,1)^T\) ✓.
Matches Problem 1: \([\mathbf v]_B=(3,1)\).
Problem 3Between two bases10 pts
\(B=\{(1,0),(1,1)\}\), \(C=\{(2,1),(1,1)\}\). Find the change-of-basis matrix \(P_{C\leftarrow B}\).
Solution \(P_B=\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\0&1\end{pmatrix}\), \(P_C=\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\1&1\end{pmatrix}\), \(\det P_C=1\), \(P_C^{-1}=\begin{pmatrix}1&-1\\-1&2\end{pmatrix}\). \(P_{C\leftarrow B}=P_C^{-1}P_B=\begin{pmatrix}1&-1\\-1&2\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\0&1\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\-1&1\end{pmatrix}\).
\(P_{C\leftarrow B}=\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\-1&1\end{pmatrix}\).
Direction matters: \(P_{C\leftarrow B}=P_C^{-1}P_B\) converts \(B\)-coords into \(C\)-coords. Swapping the order gives the inverse transformation.
Problem 4Apply the conversion8 pts
Using Problem 3, if \([\mathbf v]_B=(2,3)\), find \([\mathbf v]_C\).
Solution \([\mathbf v]_C=P_{C\leftarrow B}[\mathbf v]_B=\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\-1&1\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}2\\3\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}2\\1\end{pmatrix}\).
\([\mathbf v]_C=(2,1)\).
Sanity check via standard coords: \(\mathbf v=2(1,0)+3(1,1)=(5,3)\); and \(2(2,1)+1(1,1)=(5,3)\) ✓.
