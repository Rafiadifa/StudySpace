Spectral Theorem
Lin Alg Final25 June 2026
Quick reference. Spectral Theorem: a real symmetric matrix \(A\) is orthogonally diagonalisable, \(A=Q\Lambda Q^T\) with \(Q\) orthonormal eigenvectors and \(\Lambda\) real eigenvalues. Eigenvectors for distinct eigenvalues are automatically orthogonal.
★ Connected ProblemSymmetric matrix: spectral, definiteness, quadratic formexam-style
Let \(A=\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\1&2\end{pmatrix}\) and \(Q(\mathbf x)=\mathbf x^TA\mathbf x\).
chains: spectral decomposition · definiteness · quadratic form · application
  1. (a)
    Orthogonally diagonalise \(A=Q\Lambda Q^T\).
    Solution\(\lambda=3,(1,1);\ 1,(1,-1)\); \(Q=\tfrac1{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\1&-1\end{pmatrix},\Lambda=\operatorname{diag}(3,1)\).
    as shown.
  2. (b)
    Classify the quadratic form \(Q\).
    SolutionEigenvalues \(3,1>0\Rightarrow\) positive definite.
    Positive definite.
  3. (c)
    Give the Cholesky factor \(A=LL^T\).
    Solution\(L_{11}=\sqrt2,\ L_{21}=\tfrac1{\sqrt2},\ L_{22}=\sqrt{2-\tfrac12}=\sqrt{\tfrac32}\).
    \(L=\begin{pmatrix}\sqrt2&0\\1/\sqrt2&\sqrt{3/2}\end{pmatrix}\).
  4. (d)
    What are the singular values of \(A\)?
    Solution\(A\) symmetric positive definite ⇒ singular values \(=\) eigenvalues \(=3,1\).
    \(\sigma=3,1\).
Problem 1Orthogonal diagonalisation15 pts
\(A=\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\1&2\end{pmatrix}\). Find \(Q,\Lambda\) with \(A=Q\Lambda Q^T\).
Solution\(\lambda=3,(1,1)\); \(\lambda=1,(1,-1)\) — already orthogonal. Normalise: \(Q=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\1&-1\end{pmatrix}\), \(\Lambda=\begin{pmatrix}3&0\\0&1\end{pmatrix}\).
\(A=Q\Lambda Q^T\); \(Q\) orthonormal.
Because \(A\) is symmetric, \(Q^{-1}=Q^T\) — no inverse to compute.
Problem 2Why orthogonal eigenvectors?8 pts
Prove eigenvectors of a symmetric \(A\) for distinct \(\lambda\neq\mu\) are orthogonal.
Solution\(\lambda\langle u,v\rangle=\langle Au,v\rangle=\langle u,Av\rangle=\mu\langle u,v\rangle\). So \((\lambda-\mu)\langle u,v\rangle=0\); since \(\lambda\neq\mu\), \(\langle u,v\rangle=0\).
\((\lambda-\mu)\langle u,v\rangle=0\Rightarrow u\perp v\).
