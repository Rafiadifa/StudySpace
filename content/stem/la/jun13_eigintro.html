Q1The geometric picture
What is an eigenvector, geometrically? What does the eigenvalue tell you?
SolutionAn eigenvector is a direction that the transformation doesn't rotate — it stays on its own span. The eigenvalue is the factor by which it's stretched (or flipped, if negative) along that line.
Q2Why \(\det(A-\lambda I)=0\)?
Connect the determinant condition to the geometry.
SolutionIf \((A-\lambda I)v=0\) has a nonzero solution, the matrix \(A-\lambda I\) squashes space (some vector → 0), so it's singular — \(\det(A-\lambda I)=0\). That equation finds the stretch factors \(\lambda\) where a fixed direction exists.
