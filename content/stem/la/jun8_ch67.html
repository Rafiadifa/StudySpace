Let \(A=\begin{pmatrix}2&0&0\\0&3&1\\0&1&3\end{pmatrix}\).
chains: rank–nullity · eigenvalues · determinant · diagonalisation
  1. (a)
    Find \(\det A\) and \(\operatorname{tr}A\).
    Solution\(\det=2\cdot(9-1)=16\); \(\operatorname{tr}=8\).
    \(\det 16,\ \operatorname{tr}8\).
  2. (b)
    Find all eigenvalues.
    Solution\(2\) (top), and \(\begin{pmatrix}3&1\\1&3\end{pmatrix}\to4,2\).
    \(\lambda=2,4,2\).
  3. (c)
    Is \(A\) diagonalisable? Positive definite?
    SolutionSymmetric ⇒ orthogonally diagonalisable; eigenvalues \(4,2,2>0\Rightarrow\) positive definite.
    Yes and yes.
  4. (d)
    Give \(\operatorname{rank}A\) and \(\dim\operatorname{Nul}A\).
    SolutionAll eigenvalues nonzero ⇒ invertible: rank \(3\), nullity \(0\).
    rank 3, nullity 0.
Problem 1Determinant8 pts
\(\det\begin{pmatrix}1&2&3\\0&4&5\\1&0&6\end{pmatrix}\).
SolutionExpand along column 1: \(1\cdot\det\begin{pmatrix}4&5\\0&6\end{pmatrix}-0+1\cdot\det\begin{pmatrix}2&3\\4&5\end{pmatrix}=1(24)+1(10-12)=24-2=22\).
\(\det=22\).
Problem 2Orthogonality6 pts
Are \((1,2,2)\) and \((2,1,-2)\) orthogonal?
Solution\(\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle=2+2-4=0\) ✓.
Yes — dot product 0.
