Lin Alg Final25 June 2026
Quick reference. Eigenvalues solve \(\det(A-\lambda I)=0\). For each \(\lambda\), the eigenvectors span \(\mathrm{Nul}(A-\lambda I)\). For \(2\times2\): characteristic poly \(\lambda^2-(\mathrm{tr}A)\lambda+\det A=0\).
★ Connected ProblemTrace–determinant shortcut & definitenessexam-style
Let \(A=\begin{pmatrix}7&2\\2&4\end{pmatrix}\).
chains: trace & determinant · eigenvalues · orthogonal eigenvectors · definiteness
  1. (a)
    Use the trace and determinant to write the characteristic polynomial, then find the eigenvalues.
    Solution\(\operatorname{tr}A=11,\ \det A=24\Rightarrow\lambda^2-11\lambda+24=(\lambda-8)(\lambda-3)\).
    \(\lambda=8,\,3\).
  2. (b)
    Find the eigenvectors. What do you notice?
    Solution\(\lambda=8:(2,1);\ \lambda=3:(1,-2)\). \(A\) is symmetric, so the eigenvectors are orthogonal: \((2,1)\cdot(1,-2)=0\).
    \((2,1)\perp(1,-2)\).
  3. (c)
    Verify \(\operatorname{tr}A=\sum\lambda_i\) and \(\det A=\prod\lambda_i\).
    Solution\(8+3=11=\operatorname{tr}A\) \(\checkmark\); \(8\cdot3=24=\det A\) \(\checkmark\).
    both check out.
  4. (d)
    Is \(A\) positive definite?
    SolutionSymmetric with eigenvalues \(8,3>0\Rightarrow\) positive definite (equivalently leading minors \(7>0\) and \(24>0\)).
    positive definite.

\(A=\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\1&2\end{pmatrix}\). Find eigenvalues and eigenvectors.
Solution\(\lambda^2-4\lambda+3=0\Rightarrow\lambda=3,1\). For \(\lambda=3\): \((A-3I)=\begin{pmatrix}-1&1\\1&-1\end{pmatrix}\Rightarrow v=(1,1)\). For \(\lambda=1\): \(v=(1,-1)\).
\(\lambda=3,(1,1)\); \(\lambda=1,(1,-1)\).

\(A=\begin{pmatrix}4&1\\2&3\end{pmatrix}\).
Solution\(\lambda^2-7\lambda+10=0\Rightarrow\lambda=5,2\). \(\lambda=5\): \((A-5I)=\begin{pmatrix}-1&1\\2&-2\end{pmatrix}\Rightarrow v=(1,1)\). \(\lambda=2\): \(\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\2&1\end{pmatrix}\Rightarrow v=(1,-2)\).
\(\lambda=5,(1,1)\); \(\lambda=2,(1,-2)\).
