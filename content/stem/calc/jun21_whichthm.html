Which Theorem? — Recognition Drill
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
⌁ Decision sheet — memorise this
(1) \(\int_C\nabla f\cdot d\mathbf r\) → Gradient theorem: \(f(\text{end})-f(\text{start})\). (2) closed curve in a plane → Green. (3) closed space curve bounding a surface → Stokes (flux of \(\nabla\times\mathbf F\) through any cap — pick the flat one). (4) closed surface → Divergence theorem. Reflexes: flux of a curl through a closed surface \(=0\); \(\oint -y\,dx+x\,dy=2\cdot\text{Area}\); a radial field has zero flux through a cone aligned with it; \(\mathbf F\) conservative \(\iff\nabla\times\mathbf F=\mathbf 0\) (simply connected).
Q1scenario
\(\oint_C\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r\); \(C\) is a complicated closed curve in space, \(\mathbf F\) is messy but \(\nabla\times\mathbf F\) is simple.
UseStokes. Replace by the flux of \(\nabla\times\mathbf F\) through any surface capping \(C\) — choose the flat one. First step: compute \(\nabla\times\mathbf F\).
Q2scenario
Outward flux \(\iint_S\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf S\) through a closed surface \(S\); \(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\) is simple.
UseDivergence theorem. \(\iint_S\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf S=\iiint_V\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dV\).
Q3scenario
Flux of \(\nabla\times\mathbf F\) through a closed surface.
UseIt is \(0\) — always. (\(\nabla\cdot(\nabla\times\mathbf F)=0\), or: a closed surface has no boundary.)
Q4scenario
\(\int_C\nabla f\cdot d\mathbf r\) along some path from \(A\) to \(B\).
UseGradient theorem. \(=f(B)-f(A)\); the path does not matter.
Q5scenario
Work \(\oint_C\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r\) around a closed loop lying in a plane.
UseGreen (circulation). \(=\iint_D(Q_x-P_y)\,dA\).
Q6scenario
Outward flux of a 2-D field across a closed plane curve.
UseGreen (flux form). \(\oint_C\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n\,ds=\iint_D\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dA\).
Q7scenario
\(\oint_C\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r\); \(C\) is hard to parametrize but bounds an easy flat region (a disk lying in a plane).
UseStokes onto that flat surface. \(\mathbf n=\) the plane’s unit normal; then \(\iint_S( abla imes\mathbf F)\cdot\mathbf n\,dS\).
Q8scenario
"Is \(\mathbf F\) conservative?"
UseCheck \(\nabla\times\mathbf F=\mathbf 0\) on a simply-connected domain. If yes: path-independent, and \(\oint=0\) on every closed loop.
Q9scenario
Flux through just ONE piece of a closed surface (e.g. only the cap, or only the cone).
UseDivergence theorem on the WHOLE closed surface, then subtract the other pieces — often one piece has flux \(0\).
Q10scenario
Flux of a radial field \(\mathbf F=g(\rho)(x,y,z)\) through a cone with tip at the origin.
Use\(0\). The field points radially, which lies in the cone, so \(\mathbf F\) is tangent — no flux.
Q11scenario
You see \(\oint -y\,dx+x\,dy\) (or \(x\,dy-y\,dx\)).
Use\(=2\cdot(\text{area enclosed})\) by Green.
Q12scenario
Decompose \(\mathbf F\) into a curl-free part plus a divergence-free part.
UseHelmholtz. Find \(\phi\) with \(\nabla\cdot(\nabla\phi)=\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\); then \(\nabla\phi\) is curl-free and \(\mathbf F-\nabla\phi\) is divergence-free.
Q13scenario
A line integral \(\int_C P\,dx+Q\,dy+R\,dz\) you suspect is exact.
UseTest \(\nabla\times\mathbf F=\mathbf 0\). If exact, \(=\psi(\text{end})-\psi(\text{start})\); around a closed loop \(=0\).
Q14scenario
\(\iint_S(\nabla\times\mathbf F)\cdot\mathbf n\,dS\) over an OPEN surface with a boundary curve.
UseStokes. Turn it into the line integral \(\oint_{\partial S}\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r\).
Q15scenario
Trap: flux of \(\mathbf F\) (not its curl) through an OPEN surface, and \(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\) is messy.
UseNo shortcut. Either parametrize and integrate \(\iint\mathbf F\cdot(\Phi_u\times\Phi_v)\,du\,dv\) directly, or close the surface with an extra cap, apply the divergence theorem, and subtract the caps flux.
