Let \(z=x^2+y^2\) with \(x=r\cos\theta,\ y=r\sin\theta\).
chains: chain rule · polar substitution · double integral
  1. (a)
    Express \(z\) in \((r,\theta)\).
    Solution\(z=r^2\).
    \(z=r^2\).
  2. (b)
    Compute \(\partial z/\partial r\) by the chain rule and verify directly.
    Solution\(z_x x_r+z_y y_r=2x\cos\theta+2y\sin\theta=2r\); directly \(z_r=2r\) ✓.
    \(z_r=2r\).
  3. (c)
    Compute \(\iint_D z\,dA\) over the unit disk \(D\).
    Solution\(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^1 r^2\cdot r\,dr\,d\theta=2\pi\cdot\tfrac14=\tfrac\pi2\).
    \(\tfrac\pi2\).
Problem 1Chain rule8 pts
\(z=x^2y\), \(x=s\cos t,\ y=s\sin t\). Find \(z_s\).
Solution\(z_s=z_x x_s+z_y y_s=2xy\cos t+x^2\sin t=3s^2\cos^2t\sin t\).
\(z_s=3s^2\cos^2t\sin t\).
Problem 2Double integral8 pts
\(\iint_D x\,dA\), \(D\): \(0\le x\le1,\ 0\le y\le x^2\).
Solution\(\int_0^1\!\!\int_0^{x^2}x\,dy\,dx=\int_0^1 x^3\,dx=\tfrac14\).
\(=\tfrac14\).
