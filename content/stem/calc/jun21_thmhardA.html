★ Connected ProblemA messy field on the unit circleexam-style
Let \(\mathbf F=\big(-y+e^{x^2},\ x+\sin z,\ \arctan(xyz)\big)\) and let \(\gamma\) be the unit circle \(x^2+y^2=1\) in \(z=0\), CCW seen from \(+z\).
  1. (a)
    Compute \(\nabla\times\mathbf F\).
    Solution\(\nabla\times\mathbf F=\Big(\dfrac{xz}{1+x^2y^2z^2}-\cos z,\ \ -\dfrac{yz}{1+x^2y^2z^2},\ \ 2\Big)\). (Only the third entry, \(Q_x-P_y=1-(-1)=2\), is clean — the messy parts sit in the other two.)
    curl \(=(\cdots,\cdots,2)\).
  2. (b)
    How will you evaluate \(\oint_\gamma\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r\)? State the surface and normal.
    SolutionStokes: cap \(\gamma\) with the flat disk \(D:\ x^2+y^2\le1,\ z=0\), unit normal \(\mathbf n=(0,0,1)\) (right-hand rule with CCW-from-\(+z\)).
    Stokes; flat disk, \(\mathbf n=(0,0,1)\).
  3. (c)
    Evaluate \(\oint_\gamma\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r\).
    SolutionOnly \((\nabla\times\mathbf F)\cdot\mathbf n=\) the \(z\)-component \(=2\) survives (the others dot to \(0\)): \(\oint_\gamma\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r=\iint_D 2\,dA=2\pi\). The scary \(e^{x^2},\arctan\) never mattered.
    \(2\pi\).
  4. (d)
    Evaluate \(\oint_\gamma (e^x+y)\,dx+(x+\cos y)\,dy\).
    SolutionCheck exactness: \(\partial_x(x+\cos y)=1=\partial_y(e^x+y)\) ⇒ exact, \(=d(e^x+xy+\sin y)\). Integral of an exact form around a closed loop \(=0\).
    \(0\).
Problem 1Choose & execute8 pts
Compute the outward flux of \(\mathbf F=(x^3,y^3,z^3)\) through the unit sphere.
SolutionClosed surface ⇒ Divergence theorem. \(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F=3x^2+3y^2+3z^2=3\rho^2\). In spherical: \(\iiint 3\rho^2\,dV=3\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^{\pi}\!\!\int_0^1\rho^2\cdot\rho^2\sin\phi\,d\rho\,d\phi\,d\theta=3\big(2\pi\cdot2\cdot\tfrac15\big)=\tfrac{12\pi}{5}.\)
\(\tfrac{12\pi}{5}\).
Problem 2Choose & execute8 pts
For \(\mathbf F=(2xy+z,\ x^2+1,\ x)\), compute \(\int_C\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r\) from \((0,0,0)\) to \((1,1,1)\).
SolutionCheck \(\nabla\times\mathbf F=(0-0,\ 1-1,\ 2x-2x)=\mathbf 0\) ⇒ conservative ⇒ gradient theorem. Find \(\psi\): \(\psi_x=2xy+z\Rightarrow\psi=x^2y+xz+c(y,z)\); \(\psi_y=x^2+c_y=x^2+1\Rightarrow c=y+d(z)\); \(\psi_z=x+d=x\Rightarrow d=0\). So \(\psi=x^2y+xz+y\). Then \(\int_C=\psi(1,1,1)-\psi(0,0,0)=3-0=3\).
\(3\).
Problem 3Choose & execute8 pts
For \(\mathbf F=(x^2-y,\ x+y^2)\) and \(C\) the unit circle (CCW), find (i) the work \(\oint_C\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r\) and (ii) the outward flux \(\oint_C\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n\,ds\).
SolutionClosed plane curve ⇒ Green (both forms). Work: \(Q_x-P_y=1-(-1)=2\Rightarrow\iint_D 2\,dA=2\pi\). Flux: \(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F=2x+2y\Rightarrow\iint_D(2x+2y)\,dA=0\) (odd over the symmetric disk).
work \(=2\pi\); flux \(=0\).
