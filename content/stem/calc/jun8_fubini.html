Fubini & Iterated Integrals
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
Quick reference. Over a region \(D\), iterate one variable at a time: inner limits may depend on the outer variable, outer limits are constants. Fubini: if the integrand is absolutely integrable, \(\iint_D f\,dA\) equals either order. When the inner integral has no elementary antiderivative, swap the order and re-read the limits from a sketch of \(D\).
★ Connected ProblemA region between two curvesexam-style
Let \(D\) be bounded by \(y=x^2\) and \(y=x\), and evaluate \(\displaystyle\iint_D (x+y)\,dA\).
chains: region sketch · iterate dy dx · reverse order · Fubini check
  1. (a)
    Describe \(D\) and the intersection points.
    Solution\(x^2=x\Rightarrow x=0,1\); for \(0\le x\le1\), \(x^2\le y\le x\).
    \(0\le x\le1,\ x^2\le y\le x\).
  2. (b)
    Evaluate in order \(dy\,dx\).
    Solution\(\int_0^1\!\big[xy+\tfrac{y^2}2\big]_{x^2}^{x}dx=\int_0^1(\tfrac{3x^2}2-x^3-\tfrac{x^4}2)dx=\tfrac12-\tfrac14-\tfrac1{10}=\tfrac{3}{20}\).
    \(\tfrac3{20}\).
  3. (c)
    Set up the reverse order \(dx\,dy\).
    SolutionFor \(0\le y\le1\), \(y\le x\le\sqrt y\): \(\int_0^1\!\int_y^{\sqrt y}(x+y)\,dx\,dy\).
    limits \(y\le x\le\sqrt y\).
  4. (d)
    What does Fubini guarantee here?
    SolutionThe integrand is continuous (absolutely integrable) on the bounded \(D\), so both orders give the same value \(\tfrac3{20}\).
    Equal — Fubini applies.
Problem 1Basic iterated integrals10 pts
  1. (a)
    \(\displaystyle\int_0^1\!\!\int_0^2 (x+2y)\,dx\,dy\). 3 pts
    Solution Inner: \(\int_0^2(x+2y)dx=[\tfrac{x^2}{2}+2yx]_0^2=2+4y\). Outer: \(\int_0^1(2+4y)dy=2+2=4\).
    \(=4\).
  2. (b)
    \(\displaystyle\int_0^1\!\!\int_0^x xy\,dy\,dx\) (triangular region). 3 pts
    Solution Inner: \(\int_0^x xy\,dy=x\cdot\tfrac{x^2}{2}=\tfrac{x^3}{2}\). Outer: \(\int_0^1\tfrac{x^3}{2}dx=\tfrac18\).
    \(=\tfrac18\).
  3. (c)
    \(\displaystyle\int_0^{\pi}\!\!\int_0^1 r\sin\theta\,dr\,d\theta\). 4 pts
    Solution Inner: \(\int_0^1 r\,dr=\tfrac12\). Then \(\tfrac12\int_0^\pi\sin\theta\,d\theta=\tfrac12[-\cos\theta]_0^\pi=\tfrac12(2)=1\).
    \(=1\).
Problem 2Order swap to make it doable10 pts
\(\displaystyle\int_0^1\!\!\int_x^1 e^{y^2}\,dy\,dx\). The inner integral has no elementary form — swap.
Solution Region: \(0\le x\le y\le1\). Reorder: \(\int_0^1\!\!\int_0^y e^{y^2}dx\,dy=\int_0^1 y e^{y^2}dy=[\tfrac12 e^{y^2}]_0^1=\tfrac12(e-1)\).
\(=\tfrac12(e-1)\).
Recognising "unintegrable as written → swap" is the entire point. Always sketch \(D\) to get the new limits.
Problem 3Volume under a surface10 pts
Find the volume under \(z=4-x^2-y^2\) over the square \([0,1]\times[0,1]\).
Solution \(\int_0^1\!\!\int_0^1(4-x^2-y^2)dx\,dy\). Inner: \(\int_0^1(4-x^2-y^2)dx=4-\tfrac13-y^2\). Outer: \(\int_0^1(\tfrac{11}{3}-y^2)dy=\tfrac{11}{3}-\tfrac13=\tfrac{10}{3}\).
Volume \(=\tfrac{10}{3}\).
