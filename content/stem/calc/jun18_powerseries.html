Consider \(\displaystyle\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{x^n}{n}\).
chains: radius · endpoint tests · recognise the closed form
  1. (a)
    Radius of convergence.
    SolutionRatio \(\to|x|<1\Rightarrow R=1\).
    \(R=1\).
  2. (b)
    Test both endpoints.
    Solution\(x=1\): \(\sum 1/n\) diverges. \(x=-1\): \(\sum(-1)^n/n\) converges.
    Interval \([-1,1)\).
  3. (c)
    Recognise the sum in closed form for \(|x|<1\).
    SolutionIt is \(-\ln(1-x)\) (integrate \(\sum x^{n-1}=\tfrac1{1-x}\)).
    \(-\ln(1-x)\).

★ ChallengeExam-level · differentiate the geometric seriespast-final level
Study the power series \(\displaystyle\sum_{n\ge1} n\,x^n\).
chains: ratio test for \(R\) · differentiate \(\sum x^n\) · closed form · evaluate a number · endpoints
  1. (a)
    Radius of convergence.
    Solution\(\big|\tfrac{(n+1)x^{n+1}}{n x^n}\big|=\tfrac{n+1}{n}|x|\to|x|\); converges for \(|x|<1\).
    \(R=1\).
  2. (b)
    Find a closed form (differentiate the geometric series).
    Solution\(\sum_{n\ge0}x^n=\tfrac1{1-x}\Rightarrow\sum_{n\ge1}n x^{n-1}=\tfrac1{(1-x)^2}\); multiply by \(x\): \(\sum_{n\ge1}n x^n=\dfrac{x}{(1-x)^2}\).
    \(\dfrac{x}{(1-x)^2}\).
  3. (c)
    Evaluate \(\sum_{n\ge1} n/2^n\).
    Solution\(x=\tfrac12\): \(\dfrac{1/2}{(1/2)^2}=\dfrac{1/2}{1/4}=2\).
    \(\sum n/2^n=2\).
  4. (d)
    Do the endpoints \(x=\pm1\) converge?
    SolutionNo — the terms \(n(\pm1)^n\) do not tend to \(0\), so the \(n\)th-term test fails at both ends. Interval \(=(-1,1)\).
    diverges at both endpoints.
