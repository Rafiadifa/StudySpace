Multivariable Integrals
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
Quick reference. Double integrals over general regions: Type I \((a\le x\le b,\ g_1(x)\le y\le g_2(x))\), Type II swaps roles. Volume \(=\iint_D f\,dA\). For circular regions use polar with \(dA=r\,dr\,d\theta\).
★ Connected ProblemCentroid of a quarter diskexam-style
Let \(D\) be the quarter disk of radius \(2\) in the first quadrant.
chains: set up double integral · polar conversion · evaluate · centroid
  1. (a)
    Write \(\iint_D x\,dA\) and the area of \(D\).
    SolutionArea \(=\tfrac14\pi(2)^2=\pi\); \(\iint_D x\,dA\) to be computed.
    Area \(=\pi\).
  2. (b)
    Convert \(\iint_D x\,dA\) to polar.
    Solution\(\int_0^{\pi/2}\!\int_0^2 (r\cos\theta)\,r\,dr\,d\theta\).
    as shown.
  3. (c)
    Evaluate it.
    Solution\(\int_0^{\pi/2}\cos\theta\,d\theta\int_0^2 r^2\,dr=1\cdot\tfrac83=\tfrac83\).
    \(\tfrac83\).
  4. (d)
    Find the centroid \(\bar x\).
    Solution\(\bar x=\dfrac{1}{\text{Area}}\iint x\,dA=\dfrac{8/3}{\pi}=\dfrac{8}{3\pi}\).
    \(\bar x=\tfrac{8}{3\pi}\) (and \(\bar y\) the same by symmetry).
Problem 1Type I region10 pts
Evaluate \(\displaystyle\iint_D xy\,dA\) over the region bounded by \(y=x\), \(y=0\), \(x=2\).
Solution\(\int_0^2\!\!\int_0^x xy\,dy\,dx=\int_0^2 x\cdot\tfrac{x^2}{2}dx=\int_0^2\tfrac{x^3}{2}dx=\tfrac{16}{8}=2\).
\(=2\).
Problem 2Polar conversion10 pts
Evaluate \(\displaystyle\iint_D (x^2+y^2)\,dA\) over the disk \(x^2+y^2\le4\).
SolutionPolar: \(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^2 r^2\cdot r\,dr\,d\theta=2\pi\cdot\tfrac{r^4}{4}\big|_0^2=2\pi\cdot4=8\pi\).
\(=8\pi\).
The Jacobian \(r\) is essential — forgetting it is the classic polar error.
Problem 3Reverse the order10 pts
Evaluate \(\displaystyle\int_0^1\!\!\int_{\sqrt y}^1 \sin(x^3)\,dx\,dy\) by swapping.
SolutionRegion \(0\le y\le x^2,\ 0\le x\le1\). Swap: \(\int_0^1\!\!\int_0^{x^2}\sin(x^3)dy\,dx=\int_0^1 x^2\sin(x^3)dx=\tfrac13[-\cos(x^3)]_0^1=\tfrac13(1-\cos1)\).
\(=\tfrac13(1-\cos1)\).
