Triple Integrals: Mass & Centroid
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
Quick reference. Mass \(m=\iiint_V\delta\,dV\). Centroid \(\bar x=\tfrac1m\iiint x\,\delta\,dV\) (and \(\bar y,\bar z\)); uniform density ⇒ centroid = geometric centre. Use symmetry to zero out coordinates. Average value \(\bar f=\tfrac1{\mathrm{vol}}\iiint f\,dV\). Pick coordinates to fit the solid.
★ Connected ProblemMass & centroid of a solid hemisphereexam-style
Solid upper half of the unit ball (\(z\ge0\)), uniform density \(\delta=1\).
chains: mass · symmetry · the z-moment · centroid
  1. (a)
    Find the mass.
    Solution\(m=\) volume \(=\tfrac12\cdot\tfrac{4\pi}{3}=\tfrac{2\pi}{3}\).
    \(m=\tfrac{2\pi}{3}\).
  2. (b)
    Which centroid coordinates vanish, and why?
    Solution\(\bar x=\bar y=0\) by rotational symmetry about the \(z\)-axis.
    \(\bar x=\bar y=0\).
  3. (c)
    Compute \(\iiint_V z\,dV\).
    SolutionSpherical \(z=\rho\cos\phi\): \(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^{\pi/2}\!\!\int_0^1\rho\cos\phi\cdot\rho^2\sin\phi\,d\rho\,d\phi\,d\theta=2\pi\cdot\tfrac12\cdot\tfrac14=\tfrac\pi4\).
    \(\tfrac\pi4\).
  4. (d)
    Find \(\bar z\).
    Solution\(\bar z=\dfrac{\pi/4}{2\pi/3}=\tfrac38\) — the classic \(3R/8\).
    \(\bar z=\tfrac38\).
Problem 1Mass8 pts
Mass of the cube \([0,1]^3\) with density \(\delta=x+y+z\).
Solution\(\iiint(x+y+z)\,dV=3\cdot\tfrac12=\tfrac32\) (each term integrates to \(\tfrac12\)).
\(\tfrac32\).
Problem 2Mass (cylindrical)8 pts
Mass of the cylinder \(x^2+y^2\le1,\ 0\le z\le2\) with density \(\delta=z\).
Solution\(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^1\!\!\int_0^2 z\,r\,dz\,dr\,d\theta=2\pi\cdot\tfrac12\cdot2=2\pi\).
\(2\pi\).
Problem 3Centroid8 pts
\(\bar z\) of the uniform solid cone \(0\le z\le1,\ x^2+y^2\le z^2\).
SolutionVolume \(=\tfrac\pi3\); \(\iiint z\,dV=\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^1 z\cdot\tfrac{z^2}{2}\,dz\,d\theta=2\pi\cdot\tfrac18=\tfrac\pi4\); \(\bar z=\dfrac{\pi/4}{\pi/3}=\tfrac34\).
\(\bar z=\tfrac34\).
Problem 4Average value6 pts
Average of \(f=x^2+y^2+z^2\) over the unit ball.
Solution\(\bar f=\dfrac{\iiint\rho^2\,dV}{\mathrm{vol}}=\dfrac{4\pi/5}{4\pi/3}=\tfrac35\).
\(\tfrac35\).
