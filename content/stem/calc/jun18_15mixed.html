Work toward \(\displaystyle\int_0^1 x^3 e^{x^2}\,dx\).
chains: by parts · substitution-then-parts · definite evaluation
  1. (a)
    Find \(\int x e^x\,dx\) by parts.
    Solution\(u=x,dv=e^x dx\Rightarrow (x-1)e^x+C\).
    \((x-1)e^x+C\).
  2. (b)
    Now find \(\int x^3 e^{x^2}\,dx\) (substitute, then use part a).
    Solution\(u=x^2\): \(\tfrac12\int u e^u\,du=\tfrac12(u-1)e^u=\tfrac12(x^2-1)e^{x^2}+C\).
    \(\tfrac12(x^2-1)e^{x^2}+C\).
  3. (c)
    Evaluate the definite integral \(\int_0^1 x^3 e^{x^2}\,dx\).
    Solution\(\tfrac12[(x^2-1)e^{x^2}]_0^1=\tfrac12(0-(-1))=\tfrac12\).
    \(\tfrac12\).
Problem 1Substitution & power rule
  1. (a)

    Solution\(u=x^2\): \(\tfrac12 e^{x^2}+C\).
  2. (b)

    Solution\(u=\ln x\): \(\tfrac12(\ln x)^2+C\).
  3. (c)
    \(\displaystyle\int \frac{2x}{x^2+1}\,dx\) 2
    Solution\(\ln(x^2+1)+C\).
  4. (d)
    \(\displaystyle\int \sin^3x\cos x\,dx\) 2
    Solution\(u=\sin x\): \(\tfrac14\sin^4x+C\).
Problem 2By parts & partial fractions
  1. (a)
    \(\displaystyle\int x\cos x\,dx\) 3
    SolutionBy parts: \(x\sin x+\cos x+C\).
  2. (b)
    \(\displaystyle\int \frac{1}{x^2-4}\,dx\) 3
    Solution\(\tfrac14\ln\left|\frac{x-2}{x+2}\right|+C\).
  3. (c)
    \(\displaystyle\int x\ln x\,dx\) 3
    SolutionBy parts: \(\tfrac{x^2}{2}\ln x-\tfrac{x^2}{4}+C\).
Problem 3Definite & trig
  1. (a)

    SolutionHalf-angle: \(\tfrac{x}{2}-\tfrac{\sin2x}{4}\), eval \(=\tfrac\pi4\).
  2. (b)
    \(\displaystyle\int_0^1 \frac{1}{1+x^2}\,dx\) 2
    Solution\(\arctan x\big|_0^1=\tfrac\pi4\).
