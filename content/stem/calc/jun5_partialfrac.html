Partial Fractions + Improper Integrals
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
Quick reference. For a proper rational \(\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}\): factor \(Q\), then write one term per factor — \(\frac{A}{x-a}\) for distinct linear, \(\frac{A}{x-a}+\frac{B}{(x-a)^2}\) for repeated, \(\frac{Bx+C}{x^2+bx+c}\) for irreducible quadratics. Solve for constants by plugging roots or matching coefficients. If \(\deg P\ge\deg Q\), do polynomial division first.

Consider \(\displaystyle I=\int_1^{\infty}\frac{1}{x^2+x}\,dx\).
chains: partial fractions · antiderivative · improper limit · convergence
  1. (a)
    Decompose the integrand.
    Solution\(\dfrac{1}{x(x+1)}=\dfrac1x-\dfrac1{x+1}\).
    as shown.
  2. (b)
    Find the antiderivative.
    Solution\(\ln|x|-\ln|x+1|=\ln\left|\dfrac{x}{x+1}\right|\).
    as shown.
  3. (c)
    Evaluate from \(1\) to \(t\).
    Solution\(\ln\dfrac{t}{t+1}-\ln\dfrac12\).
    as shown.
  4. (d)
    Take \(t\to\infty\): does it converge?
    Solution\(\dfrac{t}{t+1}\to1\Rightarrow\ln\to0\); limit \(=0-\ln\tfrac12=\ln2\).
    Converges to \(\ln2\).
Problem 1 Decompose & integrate 10 pts
  1. (a)
    \(\displaystyle\int\frac{1}{x^2-1}\,dx\). 3 pts
    Solution \(\frac{1}{(x-1)(x+1)}=\frac{1/2}{x-1}-\frac{1/2}{x+1}\). Integrate: \(\tfrac12\ln|x-1|-\tfrac12\ln|x+1|+C=\tfrac12\ln\left|\frac{x-1}{x+1}\right|+C\).
    \(\tfrac12\ln\left|\dfrac{x-1}{x+1}\right|+C\).
  2. (b)
    \(\displaystyle\int\frac{3x+1}{x^2+x}\,dx\). 3 pts
    Solution \(\frac{3x+1}{x(x+1)}=\frac{A}{x}+\frac{B}{x+1}\). \(x=0:1=A\). \(x=-1:-2=-B\Rightarrow B=2\). Integrate: \(\ln|x|+2\ln|x+1|+C\).
    \(\ln|x|+2\ln|x+1|+C\).
  3. (c)
    \(\displaystyle\int\frac{x}{(x-2)^2}\,dx\) (repeated factor). 4 pts
    Solution \(\frac{x}{(x-2)^2}=\frac{A}{x-2}+\frac{B}{(x-2)^2}\). \(x=A(x-2)+B\Rightarrow A=1,\;B=2\). Integrate: \(\ln|x-2|-\frac{2}{x-2}+C\).
    \(\ln|x-2|-\dfrac{2}{x-2}+C\).
Problem 2 Irreducible quadratic 10 pts
\(\displaystyle\int\frac{2x+3}{x(x^2+1)}\,dx\). Decompose with a linear numerator over the quadratic.
Solution \(\frac{2x+3}{x(x^2+1)}=\frac{A}{x}+\frac{Bx+C}{x^2+1}\). Clearing: \(2x+3=A(x^2+1)+(Bx+C)x\). \(x=0:A=3\). Coeff \(x^2:A+B=0\Rightarrow B=-3\). Coeff \(x:C=2\). So \(\int\frac{3}{x}+\frac{-3x+2}{x^2+1}\,dx=3\ln|x|-\tfrac32\ln(x^2+1)+2\arctan x+C\).
\(3\ln|x|-\tfrac32\ln(x^2+1)+2\arctan x+C\).
Split \(\frac{-3x+2}{x^2+1}\) into \(\frac{-3x}{x^2+1}\) (gives the log) and \(\frac{2}{x^2+1}\) (gives the arctan).
Problem 3 Improper via partial fractions 10 pts
Evaluate \(\displaystyle\int_2^{\infty}\frac{1}{x^2-1}\,dx\), or show it diverges.
Solution From Problem 1(a), antiderivative \(\tfrac12\ln\left|\frac{x-1}{x+1}\right|\). Evaluate \(2\to b\): \(\tfrac12\ln\frac{b-1}{b+1}-\tfrac12\ln\frac13\). As \(b\to\infty\), \(\frac{b-1}{b+1}\to1\) so \(\ln\to0\). Limit \(=0-\tfrac12\ln\tfrac13=\tfrac12\ln3\).
Converges to \(\tfrac12\ln 3\).
Partial fractions turns a scary improper integral into a clean log difference whose limit is easy to read off.
