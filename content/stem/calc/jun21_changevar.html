Remediation · 14.6 Jacobian
Change of Variables — Hard
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
Change of variables. \(\displaystyle\iint_R f\,dA_{x,y}=\iint_{R^*} f\,\Big|\tfrac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(u,v)}\Big|\,du\,dv\). If you only have \(\tfrac{\partial(u,v)}{\partial(x,y)}\), then \(dA_{x,y}=\dfrac{1}{|\partial(u,v)/\partial(x,y)|}\,du\,dv\) (the two Jacobians are reciprocals). Never drop the Jacobian.
★ Connected ProblemA curvilinear region (hyperbolas & lines)exam-style
Let \(D\) be the first-quadrant region with \(1\le xy\le4\) and \(1\le \tfrac{y}{x}\le3\), and \(\mathbf F=(-2y^3,\ 6xy^2)\). Use \(u=xy,\ v=\tfrac{y}{x}\).
  1. (a)
    Relate \(dA_{x,y}\) and \(dA_{u,v}\); give the \((u,v)\) limits.
    Solution\(\dfrac{\partial(u,v)}{\partial(x,y)}=\det\begin{pmatrix}y&x\\-y/x^2&1/x\end{pmatrix}=\dfrac{y}{x}+\dfrac{y}{x}=2v\), so \(dA_{x,y}=\dfrac{1}{2v}\,dA_{u,v}\), with \(u\in[1,4],\ v\in[1,3]\).
    \(dA_{x,y}=\tfrac{1}{2v}dA_{u,v}\).
  2. (b)
    Find the area of \(D\).
    Solution\(\displaystyle\iint_D 1\,dA=\int_1^4\!\!\int_1^3\frac{1}{2v}\,dv\,du=\Big(\int_1^4 du\Big)\Big(\tfrac12\int_1^3\tfrac{dv}{v}\Big)=3\cdot\tfrac12\ln3=\tfrac{3}{2}\ln3.\)
    \(\tfrac{3}{2}\ln3\).
  3. (c)
    Compute \(\iint_D xy\,dA\).
    Solution\(xy=u\): \(\displaystyle\int_1^3\!\!\int_1^4 u\cdot\frac{1}{2v}\,du\,dv=\Big(\int_1^4 u\,du\Big)\Big(\tfrac12\int_1^3\tfrac{dv}{v}\Big)=\tfrac{15}{2}\cdot\tfrac12\ln3=\tfrac{15}{4}\ln3.\)
    \(\tfrac{15}{4}\ln3\).
  4. (d)
    Compute the work \(\oint_{\partial D}\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r\) (CCW).
    SolutionGreen: \(Q_x-P_y=6y^2+6y^2=12y^2=12uv\). \(\displaystyle\int_1^3\!\!\int_1^4 12uv\cdot\frac{1}{2v}\,du\,dv=\int_1^3\!\!\int_1^4 6u\,du\,dv=[3u^2]_1^4\cdot(3-1)=45\cdot2=90.\)
    \(90\).
Problem 1Linear scaling (ellipse)6 pts
Find the area of the ellipse \(\dfrac{x^2}{9}+\dfrac{y^2}{4}\le1\) using \(x=3u,\ y=2v\).
Solution\(\dfrac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(u,v)}=\det\begin{pmatrix}3&0\\0&2\end{pmatrix}=6\). The region becomes the unit disk \(u^2+v^2\le1\): area \(=\iint 6\,du\,dv=6\cdot\pi=6\pi\) \((=\pi ab)\).
\(6\pi\).
Problem 2Polar as a change of variables7 pts
Compute \(\iint_D(x^2+y^2)\,dA\) over the annulus \(1\le x^2+y^2\le4\), using \(x=r\cos\theta,\ y=r\sin\theta\).
SolutionJacobian \(\dfrac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(r,\theta)}=r\), and \(x^2+y^2=r^2\): \(\displaystyle\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_1^2 r^2\cdot r\,dr\,d\theta=2\pi\Big[\tfrac{r^4}{4}\Big]_1^2=2\pi\cdot\tfrac{15}{4}=\tfrac{15\pi}{2}.\)
\(\tfrac{15\pi}{2}\).
Problem 3A shear / linear map7 pts
Compute \(\iint_D(x-y)\,dA\) where \(D\) is the parallelogram bounded by \(x-y=0,\ x-y=2,\ x+y=0,\ x+y=4\). Use \(u=x-y,\ v=x+y\).
SolutionThen \(x=\tfrac{u+v}{2},\ y=\tfrac{v-u}{2}\), so \(\dfrac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(u,v)}=\det\begin{pmatrix}1/2&1/2\\-1/2&1/2\end{pmatrix}=\tfrac12\). With \(x-y=u\), \(u\in[0,2],v\in[0,4]\): \(\displaystyle\iint u\cdot\tfrac12\,du\,dv=\tfrac12\Big(\int_0^2 u\,du\Big)\Big(\int_0^4 dv\Big)=\tfrac12\cdot2\cdot4=4.\)
\(4\).
