Problem 1The inverse of a second derivative8 pts
Let \(f\) be twice-differentiable on \([0,\infty)\) with \(f(0)=0\) and \(f'(0)=0\). Write \(g=f'\), \(h=f''\). Since \(f(x)=\int_0^x g(t)\,dt\) and \(g(t)=\int_0^t h(s)\,ds\), we get \[ f(x)=\int_0^x\!\!\int_0^t h(s)\,ds\,dt. \]
chains: iterated integral ¬∑ region ¬∑ Fubini ¬∑ the IBP identity ¬∑ explicit solve
  1. (a)
    Sketch / describe the region of integration in the \(st\)-plane. 2 pts
    Solution The inner variable is \(s\in[0,t]\), the outer \(t\in[0,x]\). So the region is the triangle \(\{(s,t):0\le s\le t\le x\}\) ‚Äî below the line \(s=t\), above \(s=0\), left of \(t=x\).
    Triangle \(0\le s\le t\le x\).
  2. (b)
    Swap to integrate \(dt\) first (then \(ds\)), with correct limits. 2 pts
    Solution For fixed \(s\), \(t\) runs from \(s\) up to \(x\); \(s\) runs \(0\) to \(x\). \[ f(x)=\int_0^x\!\!\int_s^x h(s)\,dt\,ds=\int_0^x (x-s)\,h(s)\,ds. \]
    \(f(x)=\int_0^x (x-s)h(s)\,ds\).
  3. (c)
    Prove \(f(x)=x f'(x)-\int_0^x s\,f''(s)\,ds\). 2 pts
    Solution Split (b): \(f(x)=x\int_0^x h(s)\,ds-\int_0^x s\,h(s)\,ds\). Now \(\int_0^x h(s)\,ds=f'(x)-f'(0)=f'(x)\) and \(h=f''\), so \(f(x)=x f'(x)-\int_0^x s\,f''(s)\,ds\). \(\blacksquare\)
    The double integral is secretly integration by parts on \(\int_0^x(x-s)f''(s)\,ds\).
  4. (d)
    Take \(h(s)=e^{s}\). Use (b) to find \(f(x)\) explicitly and verify \(f''=h\), \(f(0)=f'(0)=0\). 2 pts
    Solution \(f(x)=\int_0^x(x-s)e^s\,ds=x(e^x-1)-\big[(s-1)e^s\big]_0^x=x e^x-x-\big((x-1)e^x+1\big)=e^x-x-1\). Check: \(f'=e^x-1\Rightarrow f'(0)=0\); \(f''=e^x=h\) ‚úì; \(f(0)=1-0-1=0\) ‚úì.
    \(f(x)=e^{x}-x-1\).
Problem 2A field over a hyperbolic region9 pts
Let \(\mathbf F=\begin{pmatrix}-2y^3\\6xy^2\end{pmatrix}\), and let \(\gamma\) be the boundary (CCW) of the first-quadrant region \(D\) bounded by \(xy=1,\ xy=2,\ y=x,\ y=2x\). Use \(u=xy,\ v=y/x\).
chains: Jacobian ¬∑ Green's (work) ¬∑ Green's (flux) ¬∑ Helmholtz decomposition
  1. (a)
    Relation between \(dA_{xy}\) and \(dA_{uv}\) in terms of \(u,v\). 2 pts
    Solution \(\dfrac{\partial(u,v)}{\partial(x,y)}=\begin{pmatrix}y&x\\-y/x^2&1/x\end{pmatrix}\), \(\det=\dfrac{y}{x}+\dfrac{y}{x}=2v\). So \(dA_{xy}=\dfrac{1}{2v}\,du\,dv\), with \(u\in[1,2],v\in[1,2]\).
    \(dA_{xy}=\dfrac{1}{2v}\,du\,dv\).
  2. (b)
    Compute the work \(\oint_\gamma\mathbf F\cdot d\gamma\) (CCW). 3 pts
    Solution Green's: \(\oint=\iint_D(Q_x-P_y)\,dA\), \(P=-2y^3,Q=6xy^2\Rightarrow Q_x-P_y=6y^2+6y^2=12y^2\). With \(y^2=uv\): \(\iint 12uv\cdot\tfrac{1}{2v}\,du\,dv=\iint 6u\,du\,dv=6\cdot\tfrac32\cdot1=9\).
    Work \(=9\).
  3. (c)
    Compute the flux \(\oint_\gamma\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n\,ds\) (outward positive). 2 pts
    Solution \(\oint\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n\,ds=\iint_D\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dA\). \(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F=12xy=12u\). \(\iint 12u\cdot\tfrac{1}{2v}\,du\,dv=6\int_1^2 u\,du\int_1^2\tfrac{dv}{v}=6\cdot\tfrac32\cdot\ln2=9\ln2\).
    Flux \(=9\ln2\).
  4. (d)
    Decompose \(\mathbf F=\nabla\phi+\mathbf R\) with \(\nabla\phi\) curl-free and \(\mathbf R\) divergence-free. 2 pts
    Solution Need \(\nabla^2\phi=\nabla\cdot\mathbf F=12xy\). Take \(\phi=2x^3y\): \(\nabla\phi=(6x^2y,2x^3)\), \(\nabla\cdot\nabla\phi=12xy\) ‚úì. Then \(\mathbf R=\mathbf F-\nabla\phi=(-2y^3-6x^2y,\,6xy^2-2x^3)\) has \(\nabla\cdot\mathbf R=-12xy+12xy=0\) ‚úì; \(\nabla\phi\) is a gradient (curl-free).
    \(\nabla\phi=(6x^2y,2x^3)\), \(\mathbf R=(-2y^3-6x^2y,\,6xy^2-2x^3)\).
Problem 3Stokes onto a flat disk10 pts
Let \(\gamma\) be the unit circle \(x^2+y^2=1\) in the plane \(z=0\), CCW viewed from \(+z\), and \(\mathbf F=\begin{pmatrix}-y+z^2\\ x+x^2\\ y^2+z\end{pmatrix}.\)
chains: curl ¬∑ Stokes ¬∑ flat-surface choice ¬∑ symmetry integration ¬∑ exact form
  1. (a)
    Compute \(\nabla\times\mathbf F\). 3 pts
    Solution \(\nabla\times\mathbf F=\big(\partial_y(y^2+z)-\partial_z(x+x^2),\ \partial_z(-y+z^2)-\partial_x(y^2+z),\ \partial_x(x+x^2)-\partial_y(-y+z^2)\big)=(2y,\ 2z,\ 2+2x)\).
    \(\nabla\times\mathbf F=(2y,\,2z,\,2+2x)\).
  2. (b)
    Which surface do you pick, and what is its unit normal? 2 pts
    Solution The flat disk \(S:\ x^2+y^2\le1,\ z=0\). CCW-from-\(+z\) boundary ‚áí upward normal \(\mathbf n=(0,0,1)\) by the right-hand rule.
    Flat unit disk, \(\mathbf n=(0,0,1)\).
  3. (c)
    Compute the flux of \(\nabla\times\mathbf F\) through \(S\), hence \(\oint_\gamma\mathbf F\cdot d\gamma\). 3 pts
    Solution On \(z=0\): \((\nabla\times\mathbf F)\cdot\mathbf n=2+2x\). \(\iint_S(2+2x)\,dA=2\pi+2\iint_S x\,dA=2\pi+0\) (symmetry). By Stokes, \(\oint_\gamma\mathbf F\cdot d\gamma=2\pi\).
    \(\oint_\gamma\mathbf F\cdot d\gamma=2\pi\).
  4. (d)
    Compute \(\displaystyle\oint_\gamma (\cos x+z)\,dx+2y\,dy+(x+e^{z})\,dz\). 2 pts
    Solution \(\mathbf G=(\cos x+z,2y,x+e^z)\): \(\nabla\times\mathbf G=(0,1-1,0)=\mathbf 0\) and \(\mathbf G=\nabla(\sin x+y^2+xz+e^z)\). An exact form around a closed loop integrates to \(0\).
    \(=0\).
Problem 4Divergence over an ice-cream region9 pts
Let \(\mathbf F=e^{x^2+y^2+z^2}\begin{pmatrix}x\\y\\z\end{pmatrix}\) and let \(U\) be the region between the cone \(z^2=x^2+y^2\) and the sphere \(x^2+y^2+z^2=1\), with \(z\ge0\).
chains: divergence ¬∑ spherical coords ¬∑ a slick antiderivative ¬∑ boundary flux
  1. (a)
    Compute \(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\) in terms of \(\rho=\sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}\). 2 pts
    Solution \(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F=3e^{r^2}+(x,y,z)\cdot 2(x,y,z)e^{r^2}=(3+2r^2)e^{r^2}\).
    \(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F=(3+2\rho^2)e^{\rho^2}\).
  2. (b)
    Set up \(\int_U\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dV\) in spherical coordinates; do the \(\phi,\theta\) integrals. 3 pts
    Solution Cone ‚áí \(\phi\in[0,\tfrac\pi4]\), \(\theta\in[0,2\pi]\), \(\rho\in[0,1]\). \[ \int_U\!(3+2\rho^2)e^{\rho^2}\rho^2\sin\phi\,d\rho\,d\phi\,d\theta=2\pi\Big(1-\tfrac{\sqrt2}{2}\Big)\!\int_0^1\!(3\rho^2+2\rho^4)e^{\rho^2}\,d\rho. \]
    \(=2\pi\big(1-\tfrac{\sqrt2}{2}\big)\displaystyle\int_0^1(3\rho^2+2\rho^4)e^{\rho^2}\,d\rho\).
  3. (c)
    Finish the \(\rho\)-integral and give the value. 2 pts
    Solution \(\dfrac{d}{d\rho}(\rho^3 e^{\rho^2})=(3\rho^2+2\rho^4)e^{\rho^2}\), so \(\int_0^1(3\rho^2+2\rho^4)e^{\rho^2}d\rho=[\rho^3e^{\rho^2}]_0^1=e\). Hence \(\int_U\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dV=\pi e(2-\sqrt2)\).
    \(\int_U\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dV=\pi e(2-\sqrt2)\).
    By the Divergence Theorem this also equals the outward flux through \(\partial U\).
  4. (d)
    On the spherical cap \(\rho=1\) (outward normal \(\mathbf n=(x,y,z)\)), compute the flux of \(\mathbf F\) through just the cap. 2 pts
    Solution On \(\rho=1\): \(\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n=e\,(x^2+y^2+z^2)=e\). Cap area \(=2\pi(1-\tfrac{\sqrt2}{2})\). Flux \(=e\cdot2\pi(1-\tfrac{\sqrt2}{2})=\pi e(2-\sqrt2)\).
    Cap flux \(=\pi e(2-\sqrt2)\) ‚Äî so the cone part contributes \(0\).
Problem 5A parametrized cone9 pts
The cone \(S\) is parametrized by \(\Phi(u,v)=(v\cos u,\ v\sin u,\ v)\), \(u\in[0,2\pi]\), \(v\in[0,1]\).
chains: parametric normal ¬∑ orientation ¬∑ surface area ¬∑ centroid ¬∑ flux
  1. (a)
    Compute \(\Phi_u\times\Phi_v\); does it point up or down? 2 pts
    Solution \(\Phi_u=(-v\sin u,v\cos u,0)\), \(\Phi_v=(\cos u,\sin u,1)\), cross \(=(v\cos u,\,v\sin u,\,-v)\). \(z\)-comp \(-v<0\Rightarrow\) downward.
    \((v\cos u,v\sin u,-v)\), points down.
  2. (b)
    Find the surface area of \(S\). 2 pts
    Solution \(\lVert\Phi_u\times\Phi_v\rVert=v\sqrt2\). Area \(=\int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^1 v\sqrt2\,dv\,du=2\pi\sqrt2\cdot\tfrac12=\sqrt2\,\pi\).
    Area \(=\sqrt2\,\pi\).
  3. (c)
    Find the centroid \(\bar z\) (uniform density). 3 pts
    Solution \(\iint_S z\,dS=\int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^1 v\cdot v\sqrt2\,dv\,du=2\pi\sqrt2\cdot\tfrac13=\tfrac{2\sqrt2\pi}{3}\). \(\bar z=\dfrac{2\sqrt2\pi/3}{\sqrt2\pi}=\tfrac23\).
    \(\bar z=\tfrac23\).
  4. (d)
    Flux of \(\mathbf F=(0,0,z)\) through \(S\) with the downward normal from (a). 2 pts
    Solution \(\mathbf F(\Phi)\cdot(\Phi_u\times\Phi_v)=(0,0,v)\cdot(v\cos u,v\sin u,-v)=-v^2\). Flux \(=\int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^1 -v^2\,dv\,du=-\tfrac{2\pi}{3}\).
    Flux \(=-\tfrac{2\pi}{3}\).
Problem 6Series, rearrangement & Fourier8 pts
Consider the alternating harmonic series \(\displaystyle\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n}=1-\tfrac12+\tfrac13-\tfrac14+\cdots\).
chains: convergence tests ¬∑ conditional vs absolute ¬∑ power series ¬∑ Fourier
  1. (a)
    Conditionally or absolutely convergent? Name the test. 2 pts
    Solution \(\tfrac1n\downarrow0\) ‚áí converges by the alternating series test; but \(\sum\tfrac1n\) diverges (\(p=1\)).
    Conditionally convergent.
  2. (b)
    Why can a rearrangement change the sum (Riemann)? 2 pts
    Solution Conditional convergence ‚áí the positive part \(\sum\tfrac1{2k-1}\) and negative part \(\sum\tfrac1{2k}\) each diverge. Greedily taking positives/negatives steers partial sums to any target, so a rearrangement can yield \(S\neq s\).
    Conditional ‚áí rearrangement can change the limit.
  3. (c)
    Radius & interval of convergence of \(\displaystyle\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n}(x-1)^n\). 2 pts
    Solution Ratio \(\to|x-1|<1\Rightarrow R=1\), interval \((0,2)\). At \(x=2\): \(\sum\tfrac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n}\) converges; at \(x=0\): \(-\sum\tfrac1n\) diverges.
    \(R=1\), interval \((0,2]\).
  4. (d)
    \(f\) is \(2\pi\)-periodic, \(f(x)=\pi-x\) on \([0,2\pi)\). Find \(a_n,b_n,S(0)\). 2 pts
    Solution \(a_0=\tfrac{1}{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi}(\pi-x)dx=0\); \(a_n=0\); \(b_n=\tfrac1\pi\int_0^{2\pi}(\pi-x)\sin nx\,dx=\tfrac1\pi\cdot\tfrac{2\pi}{n}=\tfrac2n\). At the jump \(x=0\): \(S(0)=\tfrac{f(0^+)+f(2\pi^-)}{2}=\tfrac{\pi+(-\pi)}{2}=0\).
    \(a_n=0,\ b_n=\tfrac2n,\ S(0)=0\).
