Arc Length & Surface Area
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
Quick reference. Arc length \(L=\int_a^b\sqrt{1+(f')^2}\,dx\) (or \(\int\sqrt{(x')^2+(y')^2}\,dt\) parametric). Surface of revolution about the x-axis: \(S=\int 2\pi y\sqrt{1+(y')^2}\,dx\).
★ Connected ProblemA parametrized curveexam-style
Consider \(\gamma(t)=(t^2,\ t^3)\), \(t\in[0,1]\).
chains: speed · arc length · surface of revolution
  1. (a)
    Find the speed \(\lVert\gamma'(t)\rVert\).
    Solution\(\gamma'=(2t,3t^2)\), \(\lVert\gamma'\rVert=\sqrt{4t^2+9t^4}=t\sqrt{4+9t^2}\).
    \(t\sqrt{4+9t^2}\).
  2. (b)
    Compute the arc length on \([0,1]\).
    Solution\(L=\int_0^1 t\sqrt{4+9t^2}\,dt\); \(u=4+9t^2\): \(=\tfrac1{27}[(4+9t^2)^{3/2}]_0^1=\tfrac{13^{3/2}-8}{27}\).
    \(L=\tfrac{13\sqrt{13}-8}{27}\).
  3. (c)
    Set up (don\'t evaluate) the area when the curve \(y=x^2,\ 0\le x\le1\) is revolved about the \(x\)-axis.
    Solution\(S=\int_0^1 2\pi x^2\sqrt{1+4x^2}\,dx\).
    as shown.
Problem 1Arc length10 pts
Find the length of \(y=\tfrac23 x^{3/2}\) from \(x=0\) to \(x=3\).
Solution\(y'=x^{1/2}\), \(1+(y')^2=1+x\). \(L=\int_0^3\sqrt{1+x}\,dx=\tfrac23(1+x)^{3/2}\big|_0^3=\tfrac23(8-1)=\tfrac{14}{3}\).
\(L=\tfrac{14}{3}\).
Problem 2Parametric length10 pts
Length of \(x=\cos t,\ y=\sin t\), \(t:0\to\pi\).
Solution\(x'=-\sin t,\ y'=\cos t\), \(\sqrt{(x')^2+(y')^2}=1\). \(L=\int_0^\pi 1\,dt=\pi\).
\(L=\pi\) (half the unit circle).
Problem 3Surface of revolution10 pts
Surface area when \(y=\sqrt x\), \(0\le x\le1\), is revolved about the x-axis.
Solution\(y'=\tfrac{1}{2\sqrt x}\), \(1+(y')^2=1+\tfrac1{4x}\). \(S=\int_0^1 2\pi\sqrt x\sqrt{1+\tfrac1{4x}}\,dx=2\pi\int_0^1\sqrt{x+\tfrac14}\,dx=2\pi\cdot\tfrac23(x+\tfrac14)^{3/2}\big|_0^1=\tfrac{4\pi}{3}\big((\tfrac54)^{3/2}-(\tfrac14)^{3/2}\big)\).
\(S=\tfrac{4\pi}{3}\left(\tfrac{5\sqrt5-1}{8}\right)=\tfrac{\pi}{6}(5\sqrt5-1)\).
