Active Recall: Definitions & Method-Triggers
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
⌁ Cue → response, no notes
For each cue, say the definition or name the method out loud before revealing. Two days out, this is the highest-yield drill.
1Trigger: closed curve in the plane, want work/circulation
AnswerGreen’s theorem — \(\oint=\iint_D(Q_x-P_y)\,dA\).
2Trigger: closed surface, want flux
AnswerDivergence theorem — \(\iint_{\partial V}=\iiint_V\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dV\).
3Trigger: closed space curve, messy \(\mathbf F\), simple \( abla imes\mathbf F\)
AnswerStokes — replace by curl-flux through any capping surface (choose the flat one).
4Trigger: which coordinates?
AnswerDisk/annulus → polar; cylinder/“around an axis” → cylindrical; ball/cone/“distance from origin” → spherical.
5Trigger: inner antiderivative impossible (e.g. \(e^{y^2}\))
AnswerSwap the order of integration (Fubini); re-describe the region.
6Define: conservative field
Answer\(\mathbf F=\nabla f\) for some potential \(f\). Equivalent (simply-connected domain): \(\nabla\times\mathbf F=\mathbf0\); line integrals path-independent; \(\oint_C\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r=0\) for every closed \(C\).
7Define: divergence & curl
Answer\(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F=P_x+Q_y+R_z\) (flux density / source strength); \(\nabla\times\mathbf F=(R_y-Q_z,\,P_z-R_x,\,Q_x-P_y)\) (circulation density).
8Define: positive orientation
AnswerBoundary curve CCW when the region is on your left / normal points out of a closed surface. Flux sign follows the chosen normal.
9When does \(\sum c_n(x-a)^n\) converge?
AnswerFor \(|x-a|<R\) where \(R=1/\limsup|c_n|^{1/n}\) (or via the ratio test); diverges for \(|x-a|>R\); endpoints tested separately.
10Identities to quote instantly
Answer\(\nabla\cdot(\nabla\times\mathbf F)=0\) (curl is divergence-free); \(\nabla\times(\nabla f)=\mathbf0\) (gradients are curl-free).
