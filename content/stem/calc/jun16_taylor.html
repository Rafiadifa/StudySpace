Improper + Taylor Series
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
Quick reference. Known expansions: \(e^x=\sum\frac{x^n}{n!}\), \(\frac{1}{1-x}=\sum x^n\ (|x|<1)\), \(\sin x=\sum\frac{(-1)^n x^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!}\). Taylor: \(f(x)=\sum\frac{f^{(n)}(a)}{n!}(x-a)^n\). Substitute into known series rather than differentiating repeatedly.
★ Connected ProblemA series that names a numberexam-style
Let \(f(x)=\ln(1+x)\).
chains: Maclaurin series · radius of convergence · evaluate at a point
  1. (a)
    Find the Maclaurin series.
    Solution\(\ln(1+x)=\sum_{n\ge1}\dfrac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n}x^n\).
    as shown.
  2. (b)
    Radius of convergence?
    SolutionRatio \(\to|x|<1\Rightarrow R=1\).
    \(R=1\).
  3. (c)
    Set \(x=1\): what number does the series give?
    Solution\(\sum\dfrac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n}=\ln2\) (series converges at \(x=1\)).
    \(\ln2\).
Problem 1Improper8 pts
\(\int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x^3}\,dx\).
Solution\(\int_1^b x^{-3}dx=[-\tfrac12 x^{-2}]_1^b=\tfrac12-\tfrac1{2b^2}\to\tfrac12\).
Converges to \(\tfrac12\).
Problem 2Maclaurin by substitution10 pts
Find the Maclaurin series of \(e^{-x^2}\) and its first three terms.
SolutionSubstitute \(-x^2\) into \(e^u=\sum u^n/n!\): \(e^{-x^2}=\sum\frac{(-1)^n x^{2n}}{n!}=1-x^2+\tfrac{x^4}{2}-\cdots\).
\(1-x^2+\tfrac{x^4}{2}-\cdots\).
Problem 3Taylor at a point8 pts
First three terms of \(\ln x\) about \(a=1\).
Solution\(f(1)=0,\ f'=1/x\Rightarrow1,\ f''=-1/x^2\Rightarrow-1\). \(\ln x=(x-1)-\tfrac12(x-1)^2+\cdots\).
\((x-1)-\tfrac12(x-1)^2+\tfrac13(x-1)^3-\cdots\).
