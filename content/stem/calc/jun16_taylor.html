Surface Integrals & Parametrized Surfaces
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
Quick reference. Parametrize \(S\) by \(\Phi(u,v)\). Area element \(dS=\lVert\Phi_u\times\Phi_v\rVert\,du\,dv\); for a graph \(z=g(x,y)\), \(dS=\sqrt{1+g_x^2+g_y^2}\,dA\). Scalar surface integral \(\iint_S f\,dS\). Flux \(\iint_S\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf S=\iint\mathbf F\cdot(\Phi_u\times\Phi_v)\,du\,dv\); for an upward graph, flux \(=\iint_D(-Pg_x-Qg_y+R)\,dA\). Watch the normal’s orientation.
★ Connected ProblemParametrized hemisphere: normal, area, fluxexam-style
Unit upper hemisphere \(S:\ \Phi(\phi,\theta)=(\sin\phi\cos\theta,\ \sin\phi\sin\theta,\ \cos\phi)\), \(\phi\in[0,\tfrac\pi2],\ \theta\in[0,2\pi]\).
chains: normal vector · area element · scalar surface integral · flux
  1. (a)
    Compute \(\Phi_\phi\times\Phi_\theta\) and \(\lVert\Phi_\phi\times\Phi_\theta\rVert\).
    Solution\(\Phi_\phi=(\cos\phi\cos\theta,\cos\phi\sin\theta,-\sin\phi)\), \(\Phi_\theta=(-\sin\phi\sin\theta,\sin\phi\cos\theta,0)\); cross \(=\sin\phi\,(\sin\phi\cos\theta,\sin\phi\sin\theta,\cos\phi)\), magnitude \(\sin\phi\). So \(dS=\sin\phi\,d\phi\,d\theta\).
    \(dS=\sin\phi\,d\phi\,d\theta\).
  2. (b)
    Find the surface area of the hemisphere.
    Solution\(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^{\pi/2}\sin\phi\,d\phi\,d\theta=2\pi\cdot1=2\pi\).
    \(2\pi\).
  3. (c)
    Compute \(\iint_S z\,dS\).
    Solution\(z=\cos\phi\): \(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^{\pi/2}\cos\phi\sin\phi\,d\phi\,d\theta=2\pi\cdot\tfrac12=\pi\).
    \(\pi\).
  4. (d)
    Flux of \(\mathbf F=(x,y,z)\) outward through \(S\).
    SolutionOn the unit sphere the outward normal is \(\mathbf n=(x,y,z)\), so \(\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n=x^2+y^2+z^2=1\); flux \(=\iint_S 1\,dS=2\pi\).
    \(2\pi\).
Problem 1Surface area (parametric)8 pts
Cone \(\Phi(u,v)=(v\cos u,\ v\sin u,\ v)\), \(u\in[0,2\pi],\ v\in[0,1]\). Find its area.
Solution\(\Phi_u\times\Phi_v=(v\cos u,v\sin u,-v)\), magnitude \(v\sqrt2\); area \(=\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^1 v\sqrt2\,dv\,du=2\pi\cdot\tfrac{\sqrt2}{2}=\sqrt2\,\pi\).
\(\sqrt2\,\pi\).
Problem 2Surface area (graph)6 pts
Area of the plane \(z=x+y\) over the unit square \([0,1]^2\).
Solution\(dS=\sqrt{1+z_x^2+z_y^2}\,dA=\sqrt{1+1+1}\,dA=\sqrt3\,dA\); area \(=\sqrt3\).
\(\sqrt3\).
Problem 3Flux through a graph8 pts
Flux of \(\mathbf F=(x,y,z)\) upward through \(z=1-x^2-y^2\), \(z\ge0\).
SolutionUpward graph: flux \(=\iint_D(-Pg_x-Qg_y+R)\,dA\) with \(g_x=-2x,g_y=-2y\): \(\iint_D(2x^2+2y^2+1-x^2-y^2)\,dA=\iint_D(1+x^2+y^2)\,dA=\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^1(1+r^2)r\,dr\,d\theta=2\pi\cdot\tfrac34=\tfrac{3\pi}{2}\).
\(\tfrac{3\pi}{2}\).
