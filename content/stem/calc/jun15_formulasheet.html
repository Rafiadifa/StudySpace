Active Recall: Calc A2 Theorems

⌁ State each result cold
Write each theorem/formula on your own sheet from memory first — reconstruction is the studying. Reveal only to check.
1Fubini’s theorem
AnswerFor continuous \(f\) on a (nice) region, \(\iint f\,dA\) equals either iterated integral — you may swap \(dx\,dy\leftrightarrow dy\,dx\) (adjusting limits). Useful when one order has no elementary antiderivative.
2Change of variables
Answer\(\iint_R f(x,y)\,dA=\iint_S f\big(x(u,v),y(u,v)\big)\,\bigl\lvert\tfrac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(u,v)}\bigr\rvert\,du\,dv\). The Jacobian factor is non-negotiable.
3Coordinate volume elements
AnswerPolar \(dA=r\,dr\,d\theta\); cylindrical \(dV=r\,dz\,dr\,d\theta\); spherical \(dV=\rho^2\sin\phi\,d\rho\,d\phi\,d\theta\) with \(x=\rho\sin\phi\cos\theta,\ z=\rho\cos\phi\).
4Gradient theorem (FTC for line integrals)
Answer\(\int_C\nabla f\cdot d\mathbf r=f(B)-f(A)\). \(\mathbf F\) is conservative \(\iff\nabla\times\mathbf F=\mathbf0\) on a simply-connected domain \(\iff\) line integrals are path-independent.
5Green’s theorem
Answer\(\oint_C\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r=\iint_D(\partial_xQ-\partial_yP)\,dA\), \(C=\partial D\) oriented CCW. Idea: the planar case of Stokes.
6Stokes’ theorem
Answer\(\oint_{\partial S}\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r=\iint_S(\nabla\times\mathbf F)\cdot d\mathbf S\). The capping surface \(S\) is your choice — pick the easiest one.
7Divergence theorem
Answer\(\iint_{\partial V}\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf S=\iiint_V\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dV\) (outward normal). Turns a closed-surface flux into a volume integral.
8Surface area / element
Answer\(dS=\lVert\Phi_u\times\Phi_v\rVert\,du\,dv\); for a graph \(z=g(x,y)\), \(dS=\sqrt{1+g_x^2+g_y^2}\,dA\). Flux: \(\iint_S\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf S=\iint\mathbf F\cdot(\Phi_u\times\Phi_v)\,du\,dv\).
9Taylor’s theorem
Answer\(f(x)=\sum_{k=0}^{n}\tfrac{f^{(k)}(a)}{k!}(x-a)^k+R_n\), remainder \(R_n=\tfrac{f^{(n+1)}(\xi)}{(n+1)!}(x-a)^{n+1}\) (Lagrange form).
10Fourier coefficients
AnswerFor \(2\pi\)-periodic \(f\): \(a_0=\tfrac1{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi}\!f\), \(a_n=\tfrac1\pi\int_0^{2\pi}\!f\cos nx\), \(b_n=\tfrac1\pi\int_0^{2\pi}\!f\sin nx\). At a jump the series converges to the midpoint.
