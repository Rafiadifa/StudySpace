Double Integrals
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
Quick reference. \(\iint_D f\,dA\) sums \(f\) over a 2-D region. Fubini: over a rectangle either order works. Type I region \(a\le x\le b,\ g_1(x)\le y\le g_2(x)\) (do \(dy\) first); Type II \(c\le y\le d,\ h_1(y)\le x\le h_2(y)\) (do \(dx\) first). If an antiderivative is impossible one way, swap the order. In polar, \(dA=r\,dr\,d\theta\).
★ Connected ProblemReverse the order to make it solvableexam-style
Evaluate \(\displaystyle I=\int_0^1\!\!\int_x^1 e^{y^2}\,dy\,dx\).
chains: sketch region · why dy-first fails · swap order · evaluate
  1. (a)
    Sketch and describe the region of integration.
    SolutionFor each \(x\in[0,1]\), \(y\) runs from \(x\) up to \(1\). So \(D=\{(x,y):0\le x\le1,\ x\le y\le1\}\): the triangle with vertices \((0,0),(0,1),(1,1)\) — above \(y=x\), below \(y=1\).
    triangle above \(y=x\), below \(y=1\).
  2. (b)
    Why can't you integrate in the given order (\(dy\) first)?
    Solution\(\int e^{y^2}\,dy\) has no elementary antiderivative, so the inner \(dy\)-integral cannot be done in closed form.
    \(e^{y^2}\) has no elementary antiderivative.
  3. (c)
    Re-describe \(D\) with \(dx\) innermost and rewrite \(I\).
    SolutionFor each \(y\in[0,1]\), \(x\) runs from \(0\) to \(y\). So \(I=\int_0^1\!\!\int_0^{y}e^{y^2}\,dx\,dy\).
    \(I=\int_0^1\!\int_0^{y}e^{y^2}\,dx\,dy\).
  4. (d)
    Evaluate \(I\).
    SolutionInner: \(\int_0^{y}e^{y^2}\,dx=y\,e^{y^2}\). Then \(I=\int_0^1 y\,e^{y^2}\,dy=\tfrac12 e^{y^2}\big|_0^1=\tfrac12(e-1)\).
    \(I=\tfrac12(e-1)\).
Problem 1Over a rectangle (Fubini)
  1. (a)
    \(\displaystyle\int_0^1\!\!\int_0^2 (x+2y)\,dy\,dx\) 3
    SolutionInner \(dy\): \(xy+y^2\big|_0^2=2x+4\); then \(\int_0^1(2x+4)\,dx=1+4=5\).
  2. (b)
    \(\displaystyle\int_1^2\!\!\int_0^1 x^2y\,dx\,dy\) 3
    SolutionInner \(dx\): \(\tfrac{x^3}{3}y\big|_0^1=\tfrac{y}{3}\); then \(\int_1^2\tfrac{y}{3}\,dy=\tfrac16 y^2\big|_1^2=\tfrac12\).
  3. (c)
    \(\displaystyle\int_0^1\!\!\int_0^{\pi/2} x\sin y\,dy\,dx\) (it separates) 2
    Solution\(=\big(\int_0^1 x\,dx\big)\big(\int_0^{\pi/2}\sin y\,dy\big)=\tfrac12\cdot1=\tfrac12\).
Problem 2Non-rectangular regions (Type I / II)
  1. (a)
    \(\displaystyle\iint_D x\,dA\), where \(D\) is bounded by \(y=x^2\) and \(y=x\) in the first quadrant. 3
    SolutionHere \(x^2\le y\le x\) for \(x\in[0,1]\): \(\int_0^1\!\!\int_{x^2}^{x}x\,dy\,dx=\int_0^1 x(x-x^2)\,dx=\int_0^1(x^2-x^3)\,dx=\tfrac13-\tfrac14=\tfrac1{12}\).
  2. (b)
    \(\displaystyle\iint_D (x+y)\,dA\), where \(D\) is the triangle \((0,0),(1,0),(1,1)\). 3
    Solution\(0\le x\le1,\ 0\le y\le x\): \(\int_0^1\!\!\int_0^{x}(x+y)\,dy\,dx=\int_0^1\big(x^2+\tfrac{x^2}{2}\big)dx=\int_0^1\tfrac{3x^2}{2}\,dx=\tfrac12\).
Problem 3Polar coordinates & converting
  1. (a)
    \(\displaystyle\iint_D e^{-(x^2+y^2)}\,dA\) over the unit disk \(x^2+y^2\le1\). 3
    SolutionPolar: \(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^1 e^{-r^2}r\,dr\,d\theta=2\pi\big[-\tfrac12 e^{-r^2}\big]_0^1=\pi(1-e^{-1})\).
  2. (b)
    \(\displaystyle\int_0^1\!\!\int_0^{\sqrt{1-x^2}}(x^2+y^2)\,dy\,dx\) — convert to polar. 3
    SolutionThe region is the first-quadrant quarter of the unit disk: \(\int_0^{\pi/2}\!\!\int_0^1 r^2\cdot r\,dr\,d\theta=\tfrac{\pi}{2}\cdot\tfrac14=\tfrac{\pi}{8}\).
