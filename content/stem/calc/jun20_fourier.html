Fourier Series Setup
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
Quick reference. Period \(2\pi\): \(f=\tfrac{a_0}{2}+\sum(a_n\cos nx+b_n\sin nx)\), with \(a_0=\tfrac1\pi\int_{-\pi}^\pi f\), \(a_n=\tfrac1\pi\int_{-\pi}^\pi f\cos nx\), \(b_n=\tfrac1\pi\int_{-\pi}^\pi f\sin nx\). Use symmetry: even \(f\Rightarrow b_n=0\); odd \(f\Rightarrow a_n=0\).
★ Connected ProblemFourier of the sawtoothexam-style
Let \(f(x)=x\) on \((-\pi,\pi)\), extended \(2\pi\)-periodically.
chains: odd symmetry · Fourier coefficients · evaluate to a known constant
  1. (a)
    Why are all \(a_n=0\)?
    Solution\(f\) is odd, so \(f(x)\cos nx\) is odd and integrates to \(0\) over \((-\pi,\pi)\).
    \(a_n=0\).
  2. (b)
    Compute \(b_n\).
    Solution\(b_n=\tfrac1\pi\int_{-\pi}^\pi x\sin nx\,dx=\tfrac{2(-1)^{n+1}}{n}\).
    \(b_n=\dfrac{2(-1)^{n+1}}{n}\).
  3. (c)
    Evaluate the series at \(x=\tfrac\pi2\) to recover Leibniz’s \(\tfrac\pi4\).
    Solution\(f(\tfrac\pi2)=\tfrac\pi2=\sum\tfrac{2(-1)^{n+1}}{n}\sin\tfrac{n\pi}2=2(1-\tfrac13+\tfrac15-\cdots)\Rightarrow 1-\tfrac13+\tfrac15-\cdots=\tfrac\pi4\).
    Leibniz: \(\tfrac\pi4\).
Problem 1Square wave (odd)12 pts
\(f(x)=-1\) on \((-\pi,0)\), \(+1\) on \((0,\pi)\). Find the Fourier series.
SolutionOdd \(\Rightarrow a_n=0\). \(b_n=\tfrac2\pi\int_0^\pi\sin nx\,dx=\tfrac{2}{n\pi}(1-\cos n\pi)=\tfrac{2}{n\pi}(1-(-1)^n)\), i.e. \(\tfrac{4}{n\pi}\) for odd \(n\), 0 for even.
\(f(x)=\tfrac4\pi\sum_{k\ge0}\frac{\sin((2k+1)x)}{2k+1}\).
Exploit odd symmetry FIRST — it kills every \(a_n\) and halves the work.
Problem 2Even function8 pts
Why does an even \(f\) have all \(b_n=0\)?
SolutionBecause \(f(x)\sin nx\) is odd·even... actually even·odd = odd, and \(\int_{-\pi}^\pi(\text{odd})=0\). So every \(b_n\) vanishes.
Even \(f\Rightarrow f\sin nx\) odd \(\Rightarrow b_n=0\).
★ ChallengeExam-level · Fourier of \(x^2\) ⇒ the Basel sumpast-final level
Let \(f(x)=x^2\) on \([-\pi,\pi]\), extended \(2\pi\)-periodically.
chains: even symmetry · coefficients · evaluate at a point · famous series
  1. (a)
    Why are all \(b_n=0\)? Find \(a_0\) and \(a_n\).
    Solution\(f\) is even ⇒ \(b_n=0\). \(a_0=\tfrac1{2\pi}\int_{-\pi}^{\pi}x^2dx=\tfrac{\pi^2}{3}\); \(a_n=\tfrac1\pi\int_{-\pi}^{\pi}x^2\cos nx\,dx=\dfrac{4(-1)^n}{n^2}\). So \(x^2=\tfrac{\pi^2}{3}+\sum_{n\ge1}\tfrac{4(-1)^n}{n^2}\cos nx\).
    \(a_0=\tfrac{\pi^2}3,\ a_n=\tfrac{4(-1)^n}{n^2}\).
  2. (b)
    Evaluate the series at \(x=\pi\) to find \(\sum_{n\ge1}1/n^2\).
    SolutionAt \(x=\pi\), \(f=\pi^2\) and \(\cos n\pi=(-1)^n\): \(\pi^2=\tfrac{\pi^2}{3}+4\sum\tfrac{(-1)^n(-1)^n}{n^2}=\tfrac{\pi^2}{3}+4\sum\tfrac1{n^2}\Rightarrow\sum\tfrac1{n^2}=\dfrac{\pi^2-\pi^2/3}{4}=\dfrac{\pi^2}{6}\).
    \(\sum 1/n^2=\pi^2/6\) (Basel).
  3. (c)
    Evaluate at \(x=0\) to find \(\sum_{n\ge1}(-1)^{n-1}/n^2\).
    SolutionAt \(x=0\), \(f=0\): \(0=\tfrac{\pi^2}{3}+4\sum\tfrac{(-1)^n}{n^2}\Rightarrow\sum\tfrac{(-1)^n}{n^2}=-\tfrac{\pi^2}{12}\), i.e. \(\sum\tfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{n^2}=\dfrac{\pi^2}{12}\).
    \(\pi^2/12\).
