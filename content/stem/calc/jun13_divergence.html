Divergence Theorem
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
Quick reference. For a closed surface bounding solid \(V\) (outward normal): \(\oiint_{\partial V}\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n\,dS=\iiint_V\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dV\). 2-D Green's: \(\oint_{\partial D}\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n\,ds=\iint_D\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dA\). Needs \(\mathbf F\in C^1\) inside — a singularity voids it.
★ Connected ProblemA cubic field through the unit sphereexam-style
Let \(\mathbf F=(x^3,\ y^3,\ z^3)\) and \(S\) the unit sphere, outward.
chains: divergence · spherical setup · Gauss · evaluation
  1. (a)
    Compute \(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\).
    Solution\(3x^2+3y^2+3z^2=3\rho^2\).
    \(3\rho^2\).
  2. (b)
    Set up the flux as a triple integral in spherical coordinates.
    Solution\(\iiint_V 3\rho^2\,dV=3\int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^\pi\!\int_0^1 \rho^2\cdot\rho^2\sin\phi\,d\rho\,d\phi\,d\theta\).
    as shown.
  3. (c)
    Evaluate.
    Solution\(3\cdot2\pi\cdot2\cdot\tfrac15=\tfrac{12\pi}{5}\).
    Flux \(=\tfrac{12\pi}{5}\).
Problem 1Flux out of a ball10 pts
Flux of \(\mathbf F=(x,y,z)\) outward through the sphere of radius 2.
Solution\(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F=3\). \(\iiint_V 3\,dV=3\cdot\tfrac43\pi(2)^3=32\pi\).
Flux \(=32\pi\).
Problem 2Cube10 pts
Flux of \(\mathbf F=(x^2,y^2,z^2)\) out of the unit cube \([0,1]^3\).
Solution\(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F=2x+2y+2z\). \(\iiint_{[0,1]^3}(2x+2y+2z)dV=3\cdot2\cdot\tfrac12=3\).
Flux \(=3\).
Problem 3Green's (2-D)10 pts
Use the divergence form of Green's theorem for \(\mathbf F=(x,y)\) over the unit disk.
Solution\(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F=2\). \(\iint_D 2\,dA=2\cdot\pi=2\pi\).
\(=2\pi\).
Sanity: \(\nabla\cdot(x,y)=2\) and disk area \(\pi\).
★ ChallengeExam-level · Divergence over an “ice-cream cone”past-final level
Let \(\mathbf F=(x,y,z)\) and let \(U\) be the solid between the cone \(z=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}\) (i.e. \(\phi=\tfrac\pi4\)) and the sphere \(x^2+y^2+z^2=1\), with \(z\ge0\).
chains: divergence · spherical set-up \((\phi\le\pi/4)\) · Gauss · split the boundary · the cone is tangent
  1. (a)
    Compute \(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\).
    Solution\(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F=1+1+1=3\).
    \(3\).
  2. (b)
    Set up \(\iiint_U\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dV\) in spherical coordinates and evaluate.
    Solution\(\displaystyle\iiint_U 3\,dV=3\!\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^{\pi/4}\!\!\int_0^1\!\rho^2\sin\phi\,d\rho\,d\phi\,d\theta=3\cdot2\pi\cdot\big(1-\tfrac{\sqrt2}{2}\big)\cdot\tfrac13\).
    \(=2\pi\big(1-\tfrac{\sqrt2}{2}\big)=\pi(2-\sqrt2)\).
  3. (c)
    By Gauss this is the outward flux through \(\partial U\) = (spherical cap) + (cone). Show the flux through the cone is \(0\).
    SolutionOn the cone the position vector \(\mathbf F=(x,y,z)=\rho\,\hat{\boldsymbol\rho}\) lies along the slant lines, i.e. it is tangent to the surface, so \(\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n=0\) everywhere on the cone.
    cone flux \(=0\).
  4. (d)
    Hence find the flux through the spherical cap, and check it against (b).
    SolutionAll the flux exits through the cap. On \(\rho=1\) the outward normal is \(\mathbf n=(x,y,z)\), so \(\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n=x^2+y^2+z^2=1\); cap area \(=\int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^{\pi/4}\sin\phi\,d\phi\,d\theta=2\pi\big(1-\tfrac{\sqrt2}{2}\big)\).
    cap flux \(=\pi(2-\sqrt2)\) — matches (b) ✓.
