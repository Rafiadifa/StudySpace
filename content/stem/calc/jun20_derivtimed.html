Let \(f(x,y)=x^3+y^3-3xy\).
chains: gradient · directional derivative · implicit differentiation
  1. (a)
    Compute \(\nabla f\).
    Solution\(f_x=3x^2-3y,\ f_y=3y^2-3x\).
    \(\nabla f=(3x^2-3y,\,3y^2-3x)\).
  2. (b)
    Directional derivative at \((2,1)\) in the direction of \((3,4)\).
    Solution\(\nabla f(2,1)=(9,-3)\); unit \(\mathbf u=(\tfrac35,\tfrac45)\): \(D=9\cdot\tfrac35+(-3)\cdot\tfrac45=3\).
    \(3\).
  3. (c)
    On a level curve \(f=c\) (where \(f_y\neq0\)), give \(dy/dx\).
    Solution\(\dfrac{dy}{dx}=-\dfrac{f_x}{f_y}=-\dfrac{3x^2-3y}{3y^2-3x}=-\dfrac{x^2-y}{y^2-x}\).
    \(-\dfrac{x^2-y}{y^2-x}\).
