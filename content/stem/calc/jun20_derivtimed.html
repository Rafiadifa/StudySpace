Series Convergence Drill
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
Quick reference. Geometric \(\sum r^n\) converges \(\iff|r|<1\). \(p\)-series \(\sum 1/n^p\) converges \(\iff p>1\). Ratio test \(L=\lim|a_{n+1}/a_n|\) (\(L<1\) conv, \(>1\) div). Alternating: terms \(\downarrow0\) ⇒ converges. Power series \(\sum c_n(x-a)^n\): radius \(R\), converges for \(|x-a|<R\), endpoints tested separately.
★ Connected ProblemConditional vs absolute, then a power seriesexam-style
Consider \(\displaystyle\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n}\) and the related series \(\displaystyle\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{x^n}{n}\).
chains: alternating test · absolute? · radius · endpoints
  1. (a)
    Does \(\sum\frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n}\) converge? Absolutely?
    Solution\(\tfrac1n\downarrow0\) ⇒ converges (alternating test); but \(\sum\tfrac1n\) diverges ⇒ conditionally convergent.
    conditional.
  2. (b)
    Radius of convergence of \(\sum\frac{x^n}{n}\)?
    SolutionRatio: \(\big|\frac{x^{n+1}/(n+1)}{x^n/n}\big|=|x|\frac{n}{n+1}\to|x|\). \(R=1\).
    \(R=1\).
  3. (c)
    Test the endpoints.
    Solution\(x=1\): \(\sum\tfrac1n\) diverges. \(x=-1\): \(\sum\tfrac{(-1)^n}{n}\) converges. Interval \([-1,1)\).
    \([-1,1)\).
  4. (d)
    Name the function it sums to.
    Solution\(\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{x^n}{n}=-\ln(1-x)\) on \([-1,1)\); at \(x=-1\) it gives \(\ln 2\).
    \(-\ln(1-x)\).
Set AConverge or diverge? (name the test)
  1. (1)
    \(\sum 1/n^2\)
    SolutionConverges — \(p\)-series, \(p=2>1\).
  2. (2)
    \(\sum 1/n\)
    SolutionDiverges — harmonic, \(p=1\).
  3. (3)
    \(\sum (-1)^n/n\)
    SolutionConverges conditionally — alternating series test.
  4. (4)
    \(\sum n/2^n\)
    SolutionConverges — ratio test, \(L=\tfrac12<1\).
  5. (5)
    \(\sum 1/\sqrt n\)
    SolutionDiverges — \(p\)-series, \(p=\tfrac12\le1\).
  6. (6)
    \(\sum 1/n!\)
    SolutionConverges — ratio test \(L=0\) (sum \(=e-1\)).
Set BRadius & interval of convergence
  1. (7)
    \(\sum x^n/n\)
    Solution\(R=1\), interval \([-1,1)\).
  2. (8)
    \(\sum x^n/n!\)
    Solution\(R=\infty\) (it is \(e^x\)).
  3. (9)
    \(\sum n!\,x^n\)
    Solution\(R=0\) — converges only at \(x=0\).
  4. (10)
    \(\sum (x-2)^n/3^n\)
    Solution\(R=3\), interval \((-1,5)\) (geometric in \(\tfrac{x-2}{3}\)).
Set CStandard Taylor series (at 0)
  1. (11)
    \(e^x=?\)
    Solution\(\sum_{n\ge0} x^n/n!\), all \(x\).
  2. (12)
    \(\dfrac{1}{1-x}=?\) and \(\ln(1+x)=?\)
    Solution\(\sum_{n\ge0}x^n\) (\(|x|<1\)); \(\ln(1+x)=\sum_{n\ge1}(-1)^{n+1}x^n/n\).
