Multivariable Integration Drill
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
Quick reference. Match the region to the coordinate system. Rectangular: \(dA=dy\,dx\), \(dV=dz\,dy\,dx\). Polar (disks/annuli): \(dA=r\,dr\,d\theta\). Cylindrical: \(dV=r\,dz\,dr\,d\theta\). Spherical (balls/cones): \(x=\rho\sin\phi\cos\theta\), \(dV=\rho^2\sin\phi\,d\rho\,d\phi\,d\theta\). Change of variables: \(dA=\lvert\det J\rvert\,du\,dv\). Never drop the Jacobian factor.
★ Connected ProblemVolume under a paraboloid (why polar)exam-style
Compute \(\displaystyle\iint_D (4-x^2-y^2)\,dA\) over \(D:\ x^2+y^2\le4\).
chains: spot the disk · polar set-up · evaluate · interpret as volume
  1. (a)
    Why convert to polar?
    Solution\(D\) is a disk of radius \(2\) and \(x^2+y^2=r^2\): the circular symmetry makes Cartesian limits ugly but polar trivial.
    disk ⇒ use polar.
  2. (b)
    Write the integral in polar.
    Solution\(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^2 (4-r^2)\,r\,dr\,d\theta\) — keep the extra \(r\) from \(dA=r\,dr\,d\theta\).
    \(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^2(4-r^2)r\,dr\,d\theta\).
  3. (c)
    Evaluate.
    SolutionInner: \(\int_0^2(4r-r^3)\,dr=[2r^2-\tfrac{r^4}{4}]_0^2=8-4=4\); times \(2\pi\) gives \(8\pi\).
    \(8\pi\).
  4. (d)
    What does the value represent?
    SolutionThe volume under the paraboloid \(z=4-x^2-y^2\) above the disk where it meets \(z=0\).
    volume \(=8\pi\).
Set ADouble integrals
  1. (1)
    \(\int_0^1\!\int_0^2 (x+y)\,dy\,dx\)
    SolutionInner: \(2x+2\); \(\int_0^1(2x+2)dx=3\).
  2. (2)
    \(\int_0^1\!\int_0^1 xy\,dy\,dx\)
    SolutionSeparates: \(\tfrac12\cdot\tfrac12=\tfrac14\).
  3. (3)
    \(\int_0^2\!\int_0^{x} 1\,dy\,dx\) (area of the region)
    Solution\(\int_0^2 x\,dx=2\) — area of a right triangle.
Set BPolar coordinates
  1. (4)
    \(\iint_D 1\,dA\), \(D\) the unit disk
    Solution\(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^1 r\,dr\,d\theta=2\pi\cdot\tfrac12=\pi\) (the area).
  2. (5)
    \(\iint_D (x^2+y^2)\,dA\), unit disk
    Solution\(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^1 r^2\cdot r\,dr\,d\theta=2\pi\cdot\tfrac14=\tfrac{\pi}{2}\).
  3. (6)
    \(\iint_D e^{x^2+y^2}\,dA\), unit disk
    Solution\(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^1 e^{r^2}r\,dr\,d\theta=2\pi\cdot\tfrac12(e-1)=\pi(e-1)\).
Set CTriple integrals
  1. (7)
    \(\iiint_{[0,1]^3}(x+y+z)\,dV\)
    SolutionBy symmetry \(3\times\tfrac12=\tfrac32\).
  2. (8)
    \(\iiint_{[0,1]^3} xyz\,dV\)
    SolutionSeparates: \((\tfrac12)^3=\tfrac18\).
  3. (9)
    \(\int_0^1\!\int_0^1\!\int_0^{x} dz\,dy\,dx\)
    SolutionInner \(=x\); \(\int_0^1\!\int_0^1 x\,dy\,dx=\int_0^1 x\,dx=\tfrac12\).
Set DCylindrical, spherical & change of variables
  1. (10)
    Volume of the cylinder \(x^2+y^2\le1,\ 0\le z\le2\) (cylindrical)
    Solution\(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^1\!\int_0^2 r\,dz\,dr\,d\theta=2\pi\cdot\tfrac12\cdot2=2\pi\) \((=\pi r^2 h)\).
  2. (11)
    Volume of the unit ball (spherical)
    Solution\(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^{\pi}\!\int_0^1 \rho^2\sin\phi\,d\rho\,d\phi\,d\theta=2\pi\cdot2\cdot\tfrac13=\tfrac{4\pi}{3}\).
  3. (12)
    Area of the ellipse \(\tfrac{x^2}{4}+\tfrac{y^2}{9}\le1\) via \(x=2u,\ y=3v\)
    SolutionJacobian \(\lvert\det J\rvert=6\); image is the unit disk: area \(=6\cdot\pi=6\pi\) \((=\pi ab)\).
  4. (13)
    \(\iiint_{\text{ball}}(x^2+y^2+z^2)\,dV\) (spherical)
    Solution\(x^2+y^2+z^2=\rho^2\): \(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^\pi\!\int_0^1 \rho^2\cdot\rho^2\sin\phi\,d\rho\,d\phi\,d\theta=2\pi\cdot2\cdot\tfrac15=\tfrac{4\pi}{5}\).
  5. (14)
    Volume of the cone \(0\le z\le1,\ x^2+y^2\le z^2\) (cylindrical)
    SolutionFor height \(z\) the disk has radius \(z\): \(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^1\!\int_0^{z} r\,dr\,dz\,d\theta=2\pi\int_0^1\tfrac{z^2}{2}dz=\tfrac{\pi}{3}\).
  6. (15)
    \(\iint_R 1\,dA\), \(R\) the image of the unit square under \(x=u+v,\ y=u-v\)
    SolutionJacobian \(\lvert\det\left(\begin{smallmatrix}1&1\\1&-1\end{smallmatrix}\right)\rvert=2\); area \(=2\cdot1=2\).
