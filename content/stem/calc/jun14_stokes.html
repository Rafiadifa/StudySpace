Curl Theorem (Stokes)
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
Quick reference. Stokes: \(\oint_{\partial S}\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r=\iint_S(\nabla\times\mathbf F)\cdot\mathbf n\,dS\). Orient by the right-hand rule. Choose the easiest surface with the given boundary — a flat disk often beats the original.
★ Connected ProblemCirculation around a triangleexam-style
Let \(\mathbf F=(y,\ z,\ x)\) and \(\gamma\) the triangle \((1,0,0)\to(0,1,0)\to(0,0,1)\to(1,0,0)\).
chains: curl · planar surface · normal · flux of curl = work
  1. (a)
    Compute \(\nabla\times\mathbf F\).
    Solution\(\nabla\times\mathbf F=(-1,-1,-1)\).
    \((-1,-1,-1)\).
  2. (b)
    Give the surface and its unit normal.
    SolutionThe flat triangle in the plane \(x+y+z=1\); \(\mathbf n=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt3}(1,1,1)\) (orientation from the boundary).
    \(\mathbf n=(1,1,1)/\sqrt3\).
  3. (c)
    The triangle has area \(\tfrac{\sqrt3}{2}\). Compute the work by Stokes.
    Solution\((\nabla\times\mathbf F)\cdot\mathbf n=-\tfrac{3}{\sqrt3}=-\sqrt3\); flux \(=-\sqrt3\cdot\tfrac{\sqrt3}{2}=-\tfrac32\).
    \(\oint_\gamma\mathbf F\cdot d\gamma=-\tfrac32\).
Problem 1Circulation via flat disk10 pts
\(\mathbf F=(-y,x,0)\), boundary = unit circle in the \(xy\)-plane, CCW.
Solution\(\nabla\times\mathbf F=(0,0,2)\). Flat disk normal \((0,0,1)\): \((\nabla\times\mathbf F)\cdot\mathbf n=2\). \(\iint_S 2\,dS=2\pi\).
Circulation \(=2\pi\).
Problem 2Compute a curl8 pts
Find \(\nabla\times\mathbf F\) for \(\mathbf F=(xy,yz,zx)\).
Solution\(\nabla\times\mathbf F=(\partial_y(zx)-\partial_z(yz),\ \partial_z(xy)-\partial_x(zx),\ \partial_x(yz)-\partial_y(xy))=(-y,-z,-x)\).
\(\nabla\times\mathbf F=(-y,-z,-x)\).
Problem 3Surface independence10 pts
Why do all surfaces sharing a boundary give the same \(\iint(\nabla\times\mathbf F)\cdot\mathbf n\,dS\)?
SolutionBecause Stokes equates that flux to the boundary line integral \(\oint_{\partial S}\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r\), which depends only on the boundary — provided \(\nabla\times\mathbf F\) is defined (no singularities) on every surface between them.
Same boundary ⇒ same circulation ⇒ same curl-flux.
