Quick reference. Work \(\int_C\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r\); conservative (\(\nabla\times\mathbf F=\mathbf0\)) ⇒ use a potential. Green \(\oint=\iint_D(Q_x-P_y)\,dA\). 2-D flux \(\oint\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n\,ds=\iint_D\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dA\). Divergence \(\iint_{\partial V}\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf S=\iiint_V\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dV\). Stokes \(\oint=\iint_S(\nabla\times\mathbf F)\cdot d\mathbf S\).

Let \(\mathbf F=(2x+y,\ x)\) and \(C\) the parabola \(y=x^2\) from \((0,0)\) to \((1,1)\).
chains: curl test · potential · gradient theorem · path-independence
  1. (a)
    Is \(\mathbf F\) conservative?
    Solution\(Q_x=1,\ P_y=1\) so \(Q_x-P_y=0\) — yes (on \(\mathbb R^2\)).
    yes.
  2. (b)
    Find a potential \(f\).
    Solution\(f_x=2x+y\Rightarrow f=x^2+xy+g(y)\); \(f_y=x+g'(y)=x\Rightarrow g'=0\). \(f=x^2+xy\).
    \(f=x^2+xy\).
  3. (c)
    Evaluate \(\int_C\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r\).
    Solution\(f(1,1)-f(0,0)=(1+1)-0=2\).
    \(2\).
  4. (d)
    Why was the parabola irrelevant?
    Solution\(\mathbf F=\nabla f\) ⇒ path-independent; only the endpoints matter.
    path-independent.
Set ALine & work integrals
  1. (1)
    \(\int_C\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r\), \(\mathbf F=(y,x)\), any path \((0,0)\to(2,3)\)
    Solution\(\mathbf F=\nabla(xy)\): \(xy\big|=6-0=6\).
  2. (2)
    \(\int_C(x\,dx+y\,dy)\), \((0,0)\to(1,2)\)
    Solution\(\tfrac12(x^2+y^2)\big|=\tfrac12(5)=\tfrac52\).
  3. (3)
    Work of \(\mathbf F=(-y,x)\) around the unit circle CCW
    SolutionGreen: \(\iint_D(1-(-1))\,dA=2\pi\) (not conservative).
Set BFlux in the plane (Green)
  1. (4)
    Outward flux of \(\mathbf F=(x,y)\) through the unit circle
    Solution\(\iint_D\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dA=\iint_D 2\,dA=2\pi\).
  2. (5)
    \(\oint_{\partial R}(x^2\,dx+xy\,dy)\), \(R=[0,1]^2\) CCW
    SolutionGreen: \(\iint_R(Q_x-P_y)\,dA=\iint_R y\,dA=\tfrac12\).
  3. (6)
    Outward flux of \(\mathbf F=(x^2,y^2)\) through \(\partial[0,1]^2\)
    Solution\(\iint(2x+2y)\,dA=2(\tfrac12)+2(\tfrac12)=2\).
Set CDivergence theorem
  1. (7)
    Outward flux of \(\mathbf F=(x,y,z)\) through the unit sphere
    Solution\(\iiint 3\,dV=3\cdot\tfrac{4\pi}{3}=4\pi\).
  2. (8)
    Outward flux of \(\mathbf F=(x,0,0)\) through the unit cube \([0,1]^3\)
    Solution\(\iiint 1\,dV=1\).
  3. (9)
    Outward flux of \(\mathbf F=(x,y,z)\) through the unit cube
    Solution\(\iiint 3\,dV=3\).
Set DCurl & Stokes
  1. (10)
    Compute \(\nabla\times\mathbf F\) for \(\mathbf F=(-y,x,0)\)
    Solution\((0,0,2)\).
  2. (11)
    \(\oint_C\mathbf F\cdot d\mathbf r\), \(\mathbf F=(-y,x,0)\), unit circle in \(z=0\) (Stokes)
    Solution\(\iint_S(0,0,2)\cdot(0,0,1)\,dA=2\pi\).
  3. (12)
    Is \(\mathbf F=(2xy,\ x^2+z^2,\ 2yz)\) conservative? If so, a potential.
    Solution\(\nabla\times\mathbf F=\mathbf0\) ⇒ yes; \(f=x^2y+yz^2\).
