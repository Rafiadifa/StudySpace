Investigate \(\displaystyle\int_0^{\infty} x^2 e^{-x}\,dx\).
chains: integration by parts (reduction) · evaluate to \(t\) · improper limit · convergence
  1. (a)
    Find an antiderivative by parts (applied twice).
    Solution\(u=x^2,\ dv=e^{-x}dx\): \(-x^2e^{-x}+\int 2x e^{-x}dx=-x^2e^{-x}-2xe^{-x}-2e^{-x}=-(x^2+2x+2)e^{-x}\).
    \(-(x^2+2x+2)e^{-x}+C\).
  2. (b)
    Evaluate from \(0\) to \(t\).
    Solution\(\big[-(x^2+2x+2)e^{-x}\big]_0^t=2-(t^2+2t+2)e^{-t}\).
    \(2-(t^2+2t+2)e^{-t}\).
  3. (c)
    Take \(t\to\infty\): does it converge?
    SolutionThe exponential beats the polynomial, so \((t^2+2t+2)e^{-t}\to0\) and the limit is \(2\). (It\'s \(\Gamma(3)=2!\).)
    Converges to \(2\).
