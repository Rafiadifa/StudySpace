Changing the Order (t, s, x)
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
⌁ The one trick that fixes all of these
Treat \(x\) as a fixed constant (a parameter), and draw the region in the \(s\!-\!t\) plane. Inner limits depend on the outer variable; outer limits are constants. \(\displaystyle\int_0^x\!\!\int_0^t(\cdots)\,ds\,dt\): outer \(t\in[0,x]\), inner \(s\in[0,t]\) → the triangle \(\{0\le s\le t\le x\}\) (below the line \(t=s\)... i.e. \(s\le t\)). To flip: read the same triangle by columns instead of rows. The collapse \(\displaystyle\int_s^x dt=(x-s)\) is what turns the double integral into a single one.
★ Worked WalkthroughRepeated integration (the exact Q1 setup)study this first
Let \(h\) be continuous and \(F(x)=\displaystyle\int_0^x\!\!\int_0^t h(s)\,ds\,dt\). (If \(h=f''\), this \(F\) is just \(f\) — "undoing" two derivatives.)
  1. (a)
    Draw / describe the region in the \(s\!-\!t\) plane (with \(x\) fixed).
    SolutionOuter \(t:0\to x\); for each \(t\), inner \(s:0\to t\). So the region is \(\{(s,t):0\le s\le t\le x\}\) — the triangle with corners \((0,0),(0,x),(x,x)\), lying above the diagonal \(t=s\) (since \(t\ge s\)), left of \(t\)-axis, under \(t=x\).
    triangle \(0\le s\le t\le x\).
  2. (b)
    Reverse the order to \(ds\) outer, \(dt\) inner.
    SolutionNow fix \(s\) first. From the triangle, \(s\) ranges \(0\to x\); for each \(s\), \(t\) goes from the diagonal \(t=s\) up to \(t=x\). So \[F(x)=\int_0^x\!\!\int_s^x h(s)\,dt\,ds.\]
    \(\int_0^x\!\int_s^x h(s)\,dt\,ds\).
  3. (c)
    Collapse the inner integral and split.
    Solution\(h(s)\) is constant in \(t\), so \(\int_s^x h(s)\,dt=h(s)(x-s)\). Thus \[F(x)=\int_0^x (x-s)\,h(s)\,ds=x\!\int_0^x h(s)\,ds-\int_0^x s\,h(s)\,ds.\] (If \(h=f'': \int_0^x h=f'(x)\), giving the Final's identity \(F=x f'(x)-\int_0^x s f''(s)\,ds\).)
    \(F=\int_0^x (x-s)h(s)\,ds\).
  4. (d)
    Evaluate when \(h(s)=e^{s}\).
    Solution\(F(x)=\int_0^x(x-s)e^s\,ds=x\!\int_0^x e^s ds-\int_0^x s e^s ds=x(e^x-1)-\big[(s-1)e^s\big]_0^x=x e^x-x-\big((x-1)e^x+1\big)=e^x-x-1.\)
    \(F(x)=e^{x}-x-1\).
Problem 1The other trianglemedium
Reverse the order and simplify \(\displaystyle\int_0^x\!\!\int_t^x g(s)\,ds\,dt\) (note the inner \(s\) now runs from \(t\) up to \(x\)).
SolutionRegion: outer \(t:0\to x\), inner \(s:t\to x\) → \(\{0\le t\le s\le x\}\) (the triangle below the diagonal, \(t\le s\)). Flip: fix \(s:0\to x\), then \(t:0\to s\). So \[\int_0^x\!\!\int_t^x g(s)\,ds\,dt=\int_0^x\!\!\int_0^s g(s)\,dt\,ds=\int_0^x s\,g(s)\,ds.\] (Quick check \(g=1\): both sides give \(\tfrac{x^2}{2}\).)
\(\int_0^x s\,g(s)\,ds\).
Problem 2Must-swap (non-elementary inner)medium
Evaluate \(\displaystyle\int_0^1\!\!\int_s^1 e^{t^2}\,dt\,ds\).
Solution\(\int e^{t^2}dt\) is not elementary, so swap. Region \(\{0\le s\le t\le1\}\): fix \(t:0\to1\), inner \(s:0\to t\). \[\int_0^1\!\!\int_0^t e^{t^2}\,ds\,dt=\int_0^1 e^{t^2}\!\cdot t\,dt=\Big[\tfrac12 e^{t^2}\Big]_0^1=\tfrac12(e-1).\]
\(\tfrac12(e-1)\).
Problem 3Swap to confirm (both orders)warm-up
Compute \(\displaystyle\int_0^2\!\!\int_s^2 s\,t\,dt\,ds\) directly, then again by reversing the order — they must match.
SolutionDirect: \(\int_0^2 s\big[\tfrac{t^2}{2}\big]_s^2 ds=\int_0^2 s\big(2-\tfrac{s^2}{2}\big)ds=\int_0^2(2s-\tfrac{s^3}{2})ds=[s^2-\tfrac{s^4}{8}]_0^2=4-2=2.\) Reversed (region \(0\le s\le t\le2\), fix \(t:0\to2\), \(s:0\to t\)): \(\int_0^2 t\big[\tfrac{s^2}{2}\big]_0^t dt=\int_0^2 \tfrac{t^3}{2}dt=[\tfrac{t^4}{8}]_0^2=2.\) ✓ Same.
\(2\) (both ways).
Problem 4★ Triple-nested (Cauchy formula)challenge
Show \(\displaystyle\int_0^x\!\!\int_0^t\!\!\int_0^s h(r)\,dr\,ds\,dt=\int_0^x \frac{(x-r)^2}{2}\,h(r)\,dr\), then evaluate it for \(h\equiv1\).
SolutionDo the inner two by the walkthrough: \(\int_0^t\!\int_0^s h\,dr\,ds=\int_0^t(t-r)h(r)\,dr\). Then the outer collapses the \(t\)-integral: swapping \(0\le r\le t\le x\) gives \(\int_0^x\!\Big(\int_r^x (t-r)\,dt\Big)h(r)\,dr=\int_0^x \tfrac{(x-r)^2}{2}h(r)\,dr\) (since \(\int_r^x(t-r)dt=\tfrac{(x-r)^2}{2}\)). For \(h\equiv1\): \(\int_0^x\tfrac{(x-r)^2}{2}dr=\big[-\tfrac{(x-r)^3}{6}\big]_0^x=\tfrac{x^3}{6}\). (This is the \(n\)-fold integral pattern \(\int_0^x\tfrac{(x-r)^{n-1}}{(n-1)!}h\,dr\).)
\(=\tfrac{x^3}{6}\) for \(h\equiv1\).
