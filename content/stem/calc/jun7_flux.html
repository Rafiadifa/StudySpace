Flux & Surface Integrals
Calc A2 Final23 June 2026
Quick reference. Flux \(=\displaystyle\iint_S \mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n\,dS\). For a graph \(z=g(x,y)\), the upward normal element is \(\mathbf n\,dS=(-g_x,-g_y,1)\,dA\). For a parametrised surface, \(\mathbf n\,dS=\mathbf r_u\times\mathbf r_v\,du\,dv\). On a closed surface, the Divergence Theorem is usually faster: \(\oiint_{\partial V}\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n\,dS=\iiint_V\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dV\). Orientation sign is the #1 error.
★ Connected ProblemFlux through a closed cylinderexam-style
Let \(\mathbf F=(x,\ y,\ 2z)\) and let \(S\) be the closed cylinder \(x^2+y^2\le1,\ 0\le z\le1\) (with both caps), outward normal.
chains: divergence · Divergence Theorem · cap flux · deduce the rest
  1. (a)
    Compute \(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\).
    Solution\(1+1+2=4\).
    \(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F=4\).
  2. (b)
    Total outward flux via the Divergence Theorem.
    Solution\(\iiint_V 4\,dV=4\cdot(\pi\cdot1)=4\pi\).
    \(4\pi\).
  3. (c)
    Flux through the top cap \(z=1\) (\(\mathbf n=(0,0,1)\)).
    Solution\(\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n=2z=2\); \(\iint 2\,dA=2\pi\).
    \(2\pi\).
  4. (d)
    The bottom cap \(z=0\) gives \(0\) flux. Deduce the side flux.
    SolutionSide \(=\) total \(-\) top \(-\) bottom \(=4\pi-2\pi-0=2\pi\).
    Side flux \(=2\pi\).
Problem 1 Flux through a graph 10 pts
\(\mathbf F=(0,0,z)\) through \(S:z=x^2+y^2\) over the unit disk, upward normal.
Solution \(\mathbf n\,dS=(-2x,-2y,1)\,dA\). \(\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n\,dS=z\,dA=(x^2+y^2)\,dA\). Polar: \(\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^1 r^2\cdot r\,dr\,d\theta=2\pi\cdot\tfrac14=\tfrac\pi2\).
Flux \(=\dfrac\pi2\).
For a graph, the \(+1\) in \((-g_x,-g_y,1)\) is the upward orientation. A downward normal would flip the sign.
Problem 2 Divergence Theorem shortcut 10 pts
Flux of \(\mathbf F=(x,y,z)\) outward through the unit sphere. Do it the fast way.
Solution \(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F=1+1+1=3\). By the Divergence Theorem, flux \(=\iiint_V 3\,dV=3\cdot\text{Vol(ball)}=3\cdot\tfrac43\pi=4\pi\).
Flux \(=4\pi\).
A closed surface + constant divergence makes the volume integral trivial — far easier than parametrising the sphere directly.
Problem 3 Flat surface flux 10 pts
Flux of \(\mathbf F=(2,3,5)\) (constant) through the disk \(x^2+y^2\le4\) in the plane \(z=0\), normal \(\mathbf n=(0,0,1)\).
Solution \(\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n=5\). Flux \(=\iint_S 5\,dS=5\cdot\text{area}=5\cdot\pi(2)^2=20\pi\).
Flux \(=20\pi\).
Only the component of \(\mathbf F\) along the normal contributes — the \(2\) and \(3\) (tangential) parts carry zero flux through a horizontal disk.
Problem 4 Open cap — close it off 10 pts
Conceptual: you want the flux of \(\mathbf F\) through an open paraboloid cap. Outline how to use the Divergence Theorem anyway, and what you must subtract.
Solution Close the surface by adding the flat disk lid \(D\). Then \(S\cup D\) is closed, so \(\oiint_{S\cup D}=\iiint_V\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dV\). Solve for the piece you want: \(\iint_S=\iiint_V\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\,dV-\iint_D\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n\,dS\).
Add a lid, apply Gauss to the closed surface, then subtract the lid's flux.
The Divergence Theorem needs a closed surface bounding a solid — the "add a lid and subtract it" trick is how you reach an open surface.
★ ChallengeExam-level · close the cylinder, then split the facespast-final level
Let \(\mathbf F=(x,\ y,\ z^2)\) and let \(S\) be the closed cylinder \(x^2+y^2\le1,\ 0\le z\le1\) (side + top + bottom), outward.
chains: divergence · Gauss total flux · curved-side flux · deduce the caps
  1. (a)
    Compute \(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F\) and the total outward flux by the divergence theorem.
    Solution\(\nabla\cdot\mathbf F=1+1+2z=2+2z\). \(\displaystyle\iiint_V(2+2z)\,dV=\int_0^1(2+2z)\,\pi\,dz=\pi[2z+z^2]_0^1=3\pi\).
    total flux \(=3\pi\).
  2. (b)
    Compute the flux through the curved side directly.
    SolutionOn the side \(\mathbf n=(x,y,0)\), so \(\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n=x^2+y^2=1\); the side area is \(2\pi r h=2\pi\). Flux \(=\iint 1\,dS=2\pi\).
    side \(=2\pi\).
  3. (c)
    Deduce the combined top + bottom flux, then check directly.
    SolutionCaps \(=\) total \(-\) side \(=3\pi-2\pi=\pi\). Check: top \(z=1,\ \mathbf n=(0,0,1),\ \mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n=z^2=1\Rightarrow\) flux \(=\pi\); bottom \(z=0,\ \mathbf n=(0,0,-1),\ \mathbf F\cdot\mathbf n=-z^2=0\Rightarrow0\). Sum \(=\pi\) ✓.
    top \(=\pi\), bottom \(=0\).
